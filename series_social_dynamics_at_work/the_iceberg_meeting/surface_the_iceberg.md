# Surface the Iceberg — Build the Argument Before the Room Commits

Use this prompt to prepare the thirty minutes you need in a meeting where momentum is already running — so when the moment arrives, the argument is already built and the most likely objection is already addressed.

---

```
Here is how [PROJECT/DECISION] looks from the surface — what the room currently sees and believes: [DESCRIBE WHAT THE ROOM SEES — the diagram, the timeline, the apparent logic].

Here is what I know is underneath: [THE REAL COMPLEXITY, EDGE CASES, RISKS — be specific about what you found in the detail that contradicts or complicates the surface view].

The room has already started nodding. Help me explain the gap in a way that's fast, specific, and impossible to dismiss — and tell me what the most likely objection will be so I can address it before it's raised.
```

---

**How to use:**

The specificity of what you put under "what I know is underneath" determines the quality of what comes back. Vague risks produce vague warnings. Specific edge cases — "the integration point between the new service and the legacy billing system will fail under concurrent load above 200 requests per second, which the timeline assumes won't happen until month seven" — produce arguments that are impossible to dismiss without engaging with the substance.

Two things to get from the response:
1. **The explanation:** how to walk the room through the gap in a way that lands fast. You have thirty minutes and a room that has already decided.
2. **The pre-empted objection:** the most likely pushback, and how to address it before it is raised. The person who surfaces the objection themselves controls the framing.

This is not about slowing things down. It is about buying the thirty minutes that saves the six months.
