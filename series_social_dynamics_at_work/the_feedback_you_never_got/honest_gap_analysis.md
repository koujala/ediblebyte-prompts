# Honest Gap Analysis — The Feedback Most People Never Receive

Use this prompt to get a direct, unfiltered evaluation of your work — focused on what is missing, what lands poorly, and what the gap is between your intention and its likely effect. The AI has no relationship to protect, which means it does not soften what it says.

---

```
Review [THIS PIECE OF WORK / APPROACH / COMMUNICATION — paste it or describe it in detail].

Here's what I was trying to achieve and for whom: [CONTEXT AND INTENDED AUDIENCE].

Don't tell me what works. Tell me what's missing, what lands poorly, and what the gap is between my intention and its likely effect on the actual audience. Be as direct as someone who cares about the outcome but has no relationship to protect.
```

---

**How to use:**

The instruction "don't tell me what works" is load-bearing. Without it, most AI responses default to a balanced sandwich — two positives around one negative — because that is what most people implicitly want. This prompt removes that buffer.

Provide enough context about the intended audience that the AI can evaluate the gap specifically. "My manager" is not enough. "A VP of Engineering who has seen fifteen architecture proposals and is skeptical of estimates" gives the AI something to actually calibrate against.

After you read the response: the discomfort is the point. Most people have never had this conversation. The patterns it identifies are the feedback that has been sitting in a drawer for years.

**Follow-up:** Ask *"What is the single thing I most need to change, and what would the improved version look like?"* — then make the AI show you, not just tell you.
