# Strategic Silence — Negotiation Roleplay with Immediate Feedback

Use this prompt to practice silence as a deliberate tool in a safe environment where slipping back into reflex costs nothing. The AI plays the other side and stops the simulation the moment you fill silence instead of holding it.

---

```
Simulate a high-stakes [NEGOTIATION/CONVERSATION — describe the specific scenario: e.g. "salary negotiation with a hiring manager", "scope discussion with a difficult client", "budget conversation with my manager"].

Play the other side. Give me your opening position, then go completely silent.

If I speak first to fill the silence, stop the roleplay immediately — tell me exactly what I revealed, what I conceded, and what a more disciplined response would have looked like.

After we finish, give me a summary of where I held and where I broke.
```

---

**How to use:**

Describe the scenario in enough detail that the AI can play a credible counterpart — their role, what they want, what leverage they have. Generic scenarios produce generic pressure; specific ones produce the real discomfort you need to practice with.

When the simulation runs:
- Make your opening move and stop
- If the AI goes silent: hold it. Count to ten if you have to. Do not add qualifications, soften the offer, or ask if they understood
- If you break — learn from the debrief. The AI will tell you exactly what you gave away and why it matters

The debrief at the end is the most valuable part. Read it carefully. The pattern of where you broke is the thing to work on.

**Variation:** Run the scenario twice — once as yourself, once instructed to "say nothing after your opening offer for at least two full minutes of silence." Compare what you concede in each version.
