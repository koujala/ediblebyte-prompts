# Stress-Test a Piece of Feedback Against Its Source

Use this prompt before absorbing criticism — not to validate yourself, but to separate structural issues from surface preferences and weight the input appropriately based on where it actually comes from.

---

```
I received this feedback: [PASTE THE FEEDBACK VERBATIM].

It came from [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF THE SOURCE — their relationship to your work, their domain expertise, their stake in the outcome].

Is this a fundamental structural issue or a surface preference? What would someone with genuine domain expertise say about this — and how much weight should I give it based on where it's actually coming from?
```

---

**How to use:**

Fill in the feedback word-for-word — paraphrasing it changes the analysis. For the source description, be honest: are they a peer in your domain with skin in the game, or someone who watched from the outside for ten minutes?

The three distinctions to listen for in the response:
1. **Structural weakness** — something that fundamentally undermines what you are trying to do. Take this seriously regardless of source.
2. **Stylistic preference** — a valid alternative approach, not an error. Weight this by how much the source understands your context and goals.
3. **Uninformed opinion** — commentary with no expertise behind it. Note the observation, file it proportionally, move on.

Building this discernment practice takes about thirty seconds. Not absorbing the wrong signal saves considerably more.
