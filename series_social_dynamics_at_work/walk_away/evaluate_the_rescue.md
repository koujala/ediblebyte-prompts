# Evaluate the Rescue — Before You Answer the Call

Use this prompt before stepping in to fix a crisis someone else created — to trace the pattern that produced it and identify who is insulated from the consequences of their decisions if you resolve it.

---

```
Here is a crisis I've been asked to help fix: [SITUATION — describe what happened and what you are being asked to do].

Here's the pattern of how it came to exist: [WHAT YOU KNOW ABOUT THE DECISIONS THAT LED HERE — who made them, when, and what accountability existed at the time].

If I resolve this — who specifically is insulated from the consequence of their decisions, and what does that teach them? What would need to be true — what accountability would need to be in place — for this rescue to be worth making?
```

---

**How to use:**

The pattern question is the part most people skip when a crisis is live and the pressure to act is real. Tracing how the situation came to exist — and who made the decisions that produced it — is not a blame exercise. It is a structural question: if you fix this, what changes? Who learns what?

The two outputs to focus on:
1. **Who is insulated:** the specific people whose decisions produced the crisis and who will not experience the consequence if you resolve it. This is not always the most senior person — it is whoever made the choices that led here.
2. **What accountability would need to exist:** the conditions under which the rescue is worth making — because the consequence will land where it belongs, or because structural change is part of what you are being asked to deliver.

Walking away is not cruelty. It is the only honest feedback some systems will ever receive. Use this prompt to make that decision deliberately, not by default.
