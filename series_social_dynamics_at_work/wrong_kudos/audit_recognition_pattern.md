# Audit Your Recognition Pattern — What Has It Been Teaching?

Use this prompt before the next recognition moment to examine what the existing pattern has been rewarding — and design a correction that reinforces the right behaviour without being disruptive to implement.

---

```
Here is how recognition has been distributed in my team over the past [PERIOD — e.g. "six months", "last two performance cycles"]: [DESCRIBE THE PATTERN — who got recognised, for what, and who actually did the work].

Here's what I want the team to produce: [OUTCOMES — be specific about what good looks like].

Tell me directly: what behaviour is this recognition pattern actually teaching — and what would a corrected pattern look like that reinforces the right things without being disruptive to implement?
```

---

**How to use:**

The description of who got recognised and who actually did the work needs to be honest. The AI can only audit what you surface. If you describe a pattern that has no misalignment, the output will not be useful.

The question "what is this pattern actually teaching" is separate from intent. Recognition patterns teach regardless of whether the people giving recognition understand the lesson being delivered. The high performers in the room are watching every award given and drawing conclusions about what the organisation values.

The constraint "without being disruptive to implement" matters. A corrected recognition pattern that requires dismantling the existing awards structure will not happen. One that means naming the person who actually built the thing, or including a "contribution" nomination alongside a "visibility" award, can happen next month.

**Follow-up for leaders:** Ask the AI to help you draft the recognition you would give at the next team moment that specifically acknowledges the work that would not have been recognised under the old pattern.
