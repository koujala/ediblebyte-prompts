# Investigate the Felt Sense — Find the Cause, Not the Dismissal

Use this prompt when you have a strong gut feeling that something is wrong but the rational arguments for proceeding are solid. The goal is to find the cause of the signal rather than override it with the better-articulated arguments on the other side.

---

```
I have a strong felt sense that something is wrong with [SITUATION — describe it specifically], but the rational arguments for proceeding are [LIST THE MAIN ONES].

Don't help me dismiss the discomfort. Help me find the cause. If this feeling turned out to be correct, what specifically would explain it — and what would I need to examine more closely before I override it?
```

---

**How to use:**

The instruction "don't help me dismiss the discomfort" is load-bearing. Without it, AI defaults to balanced analysis that usually concludes in favour of the better-documented position — which is the rational case for proceeding. This prompt inverts that: it asks the AI to take the felt sense seriously and find what might be driving it.

The felt sense is pattern recognition — your experience assembling faster than your analysis can. It is not always right. But when it signals discomfort without obvious cause, there is almost always a cause. Finding it takes longer than overriding it.

After reading the response: look for the thing the AI surfaces that you recognised but had set aside. The answer is usually in the explanation of why it should be ignored.

**When not to use this:** If the felt sense is anxiety about a genuinely unfamiliar situation rather than a specific signal of wrongness, that is a different problem. This prompt is for the feeling that something specifically is off — not general discomfort with uncertainty.
