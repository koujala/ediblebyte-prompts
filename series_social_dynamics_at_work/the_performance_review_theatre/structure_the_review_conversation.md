# Structure the Review Conversation Around the Full Year

Use this prompt when you had a strong year overall but a difficult recent quarter, and you want to frame the conversation in a way that centres the evidence rather than sounds defensive about the recent period.

---

```
My performance review is coming up. My concern is that I had a strong year overall but a difficult last quarter, and recency bias will shape how I am evaluated.

Help me structure the conversation: how do I make the full year legible without sounding defensive about the recent period? What is the right framing that centres the evidence rather than the narrative?
```

---

**How to use:**

Provide the specifics: what you delivered across the year, what happened in the recent period, and what your relationship with the reviewer is like. The more the AI knows about the actual shape of your year, the more useful the framing guidance will be.

The tension the prompt is navigating: acknowledging the difficult quarter without letting it become the story of the year. Defensive framing makes the reviewer more aware of the problem, not less. The alternative is to lead with the year's evidence and let the recent quarter appear in context, not in opposition.

**Follow-up:** Ask the AI to roleplay as a reviewer who has been shaped by the recent quarter and ask your framing questions back to you. See if the evidence you prepared lands the way you intended.
