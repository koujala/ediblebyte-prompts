# Build Your Contribution Record Before Review Season

Use this prompt throughout the year — not during review prep — to document work in specific, evidence-based terms that survive recency bias. A twelve-month log that already exists cannot be distorted by the last quarter.

---

```
I want to document my contributions from the past [TIME PERIOD] before my performance review. Here are the things I worked on: [LIST THEM ROUGHLY — a brief description of each project, problem solved, or decision made].

Help me turn each one into a specific, evidence-based bullet that captures what I did, what decision I made or problem I solved, and what the outcome or impact was. Make them concrete enough that a reviewer who was not watching closely can understand the significance.
```

---

**How to use:**

Run this prompt quarterly, not annually. The goal is a living document that predates the review conversation — a record you already have when the review season opens, not something you are reconstructing under time pressure.

The format to target for each bullet:
- **What you did** — the specific action or decision, not the general category
- **Why it mattered** — the problem it solved or the risk it mitigated
- **What happened as a result** — the measurable or observable outcome

"Onboarded new engineers" is not a contribution bullet. "Onboarded three engineers to the billing service, reducing their time-to-first-PR from six weeks to two" is.

Bring this document into your review conversation. It makes the full year legible to a reviewer who has only been watching October.
