# Extract the Signal From the Delivery

Use this prompt when something was said in a meeting — by you or by someone else — and was dismissed because of how it was said. The goal is to find the actual substantive concern before it disappears into the conversation about tone.

---

```
Someone raised a concern in a meeting and it was dismissed because of how they said it. Here is what they said: [DESCRIBE WHAT WAS SAID — as close to verbatim as possible].

Help me extract the actual substantive concern from the delivery. Is there something real here that got lost? If so, how would I surface it in a way that keeps the focus on the content?
```

---

**How to use:**

This prompt works equally well when the person who was dismissed is you or someone else. It separates the message from the messenger — not to excuse poor delivery, but to make sure the content gets a fair hearing before the conversation moves on.

The question to ask yourself after reading the response: was the tone critique legitimate feedback about communication, or was it the first move in redirecting away from the substance? Both can be true simultaneously — the delivery can be improved and the content can be important. The problem is when addressing the first is used to avoid the second.

**For leaders:** if this prompt regularly surfaces real concerns that were dismissed in your meetings, you have a room that has learned silence is safer. That is worth examining separately from any individual incident.
