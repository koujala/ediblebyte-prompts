# Test Your Substance Before the Room — And Reframe It to Stick

Use this prompt when you have something important to raise but it was dismissed as "too blunt" last time — to verify the concern is real and reframe it in a way that makes the content harder to dismiss before tone becomes the story.

---

```
I have something I need to raise at work but the last time I raised something similar it was dismissed as "too blunt." Here is what I want to say: [STATE IT PLAINLY — do not soften it here].

First, tell me honestly whether the substance holds up — is the concern real? Then help me reframe it in a way that makes the content harder to dismiss: specific, evidenced, and framed as a question or observation rather than a complaint.
```

---

**How to use:**

State it plainly in the prompt. The AI evaluating a softened version cannot tell you whether the unfiltered concern is real. The point of this step is to get an honest assessment of the substance before worrying about the delivery.

The reframe the prompt asks for targets a specific goal: making the content harder to dismiss without engaging with it. That means:
- **Specific and evidenced:** "The architecture will fail under concurrent load above X" rather than "I have concerns about scalability"
- **Question or observation rather than complaint:** "What happens to the integration point when we hit 200 concurrent users?" lands differently than "This approach is risky"
- **Already in writing before the meeting:** a concern that exists in the record before the room assembles is harder to redirect into a conversation about delivery

The substance getting a fair hearing is the outcome. The reframe is the tool.
