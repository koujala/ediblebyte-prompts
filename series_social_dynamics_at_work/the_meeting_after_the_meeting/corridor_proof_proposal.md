# Make Your Proposal Corridor-Proof

Use this prompt when a well-reasoned proposal has not gained traction — to diagnose why it is being dismissed informally, then rebuild it as a written pre-read that forces the decision into the formal record where proximity matters less.

---

```
I have a proposal that has not gained traction despite being well-reasoned. Here it is: [SUMMARISE YOUR PROPOSAL].

Help me identify why it might be getting dismissed informally before it reaches a formal decision. Then help me rewrite it as a one-page pre-read that is specific, concrete, and hard to dismiss without engaging with the substance. The goal is to make the decision happen in the formal record rather than in a conversation I was not part of.
```

---

**How to use:**

The diagnostic question — "why might it be getting dismissed informally" — is the part most people skip. Read the AI's answer to this carefully before the rewrite. Informal dismissals usually happen for one of three reasons: the framing triggers a status concern, the proposal lacks a specific ask, or the decision-maker has not been given a reason to prioritise it.

A corridor-proof pre-read has these properties:
- Specific enough that dismissing it requires engaging with the substance
- Clear about what decision is being requested and by when
- Circulated before the meeting, so it exists in the record before the room assembles

The goal is not to bypass the corridor conversation — it is to ensure the formal record cannot simply absorb whatever was decided informally without anyone having to account for it.
