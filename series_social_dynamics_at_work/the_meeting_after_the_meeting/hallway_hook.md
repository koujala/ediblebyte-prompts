# The Hallway Hook — Sixty Seconds to Make the Full Conversation Feel Necessary

Use this prompt when you have brief, informal access to a decision-maker — a hallway, a coffee before a meeting — and need them to want to engage with the full proposal.

---

```
I need a decision-maker to understand [MY PROPOSAL/CONCERN — describe it briefly] but I only have sixty seconds of informal conversation — a hallway, a coffee, before a meeting.

Help me write the one to three sentences that make them curious enough to engage. Not a pitch — a hook that makes the full conversation feel necessary.
```

---

**How to use:**

The distinction between a pitch and a hook is the thing this prompt is trying to navigate. A pitch tries to convince in sixty seconds. A hook makes the listener feel that not engaging fully would leave something unresolved.

Hooks usually work by:
- Naming a tension the decision-maker already feels ("You know how the [PROBLEM] keeps coming back?")
- Making a specific, counterintuitive claim that requires explanation ("The reason [PROPOSAL] keeps stalling isn't the cost — it's how it's been framed")
- Creating a clean, answerable question ("What if the reason [OUTCOME] isn't happening is [UNEXPECTED CAUSE]?")

Test the hook before you use it: does it make you want to hear the next sentence? If not, ask the AI to try a different angle.
