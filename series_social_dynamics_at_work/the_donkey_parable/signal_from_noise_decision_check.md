# Signal From Noise — Challenge a Decision Before the Crowd Gets Involved

Use this prompt to surface the real structural weaknesses in a decision before the commentary starts — from a challenge that has no ego in the outcome, no unlived choices to project, and no stake in which way you go.

---

```
I'm considering [DECISION — be specific about what you are choosing between or committing to].

Here's the context: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF THE STAKES, CONSTRAINTS, AND WHAT MATTERS MOST TO YOU].

Don't validate the direction I'm already leaning. Find the real structural weaknesses — not surface preferences. Tell me what a thoughtful person with actual skin in the outcome would challenge, and what a detached critic with no agenda would say that neither I nor my supporters would think to raise.
```

---

**How to use:**

The prompt only works if you tell it the direction you are already leaning. An AI that does not know which way you are headed cannot push back on it. Do not hedge the input — say clearly what you are inclined to do.

The two challenges it surfaces are different in kind:
- **The invested challenge:** what someone who cares about your outcome and understands your context would push back on — the thing a good mentor would say
- **The detached challenge:** what a rigorous, agenda-free observer would identify that your supporters (who share your investment) would not raise

Use the second one especially. The crowd does not give you this. They give you commentary shaped by their own choices and preferences. This does not.
