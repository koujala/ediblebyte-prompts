# Surface the Invisible Impact — Work That Does Not Show in Standard Metrics

Use this prompt to articulate the downstream impact of work that your team's activity metrics cannot capture — the prevented incident, the architectural decision that saved months, the root cause fixed rather than the symptoms treated.

---

```
I do work that does not show up well in [THE METRICS MY TEAM USES — tickets, commits, lines of code, sprint velocity, etc.]. Here are some examples of what I actually do: [DESCRIBE 2-3 SPECIFIC CONTRIBUTIONS — be concrete about what you did and what it prevented or enabled].

Help me quantify or articulate the downstream impact of this work in terms that a manager focused on standard metrics can understand. What is the business case for work that prevents problems rather than solves them?
```

---

**How to use:**

The examples you provide need to be specific enough that the downstream impact is traceable. "I improved the architecture" produces nothing. "I refactored the authentication service so that it no longer fails silently under load — the previous failure mode was causing intermittent login errors that generated roughly 30 support tickets a month" gives the AI something to work with.

The output should make the invisible legible — not by attacking the existing metrics, but by translating the work into the language the measurement system does use. Defects prevented, rework avoided, hours not spent on recurring incidents.

This document does not fix the measurement system. It gives you a record that survives the review cycle and makes your case in terms the system can recognise.
