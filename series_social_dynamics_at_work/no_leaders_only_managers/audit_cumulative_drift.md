# Audit the Cumulative Pattern of Professional Drift

Use this prompt to examine the sum of your professional adjustments — not the justification for any single one, but what all of them together have added up to. The individual adjustments felt like pragmatism. The question is what the pattern reveals.

---

```
Here are the adjustments I've made to how I work over the past [TIME PERIOD — e.g. "two years", "since joining this company"]: [LIST THEM SPECIFICALLY — e.g. "I stopped asking the uncomfortable question in team reviews", "I softened a rating from 2 to 3 to avoid the conversation", "I accepted a rescue I knew shouldn't be made"].

Look at the pattern — not the justification for each one individually. Am I growing and adapting, or gradually adjusting away from something I used to be?

What does this pattern suggest about what the environment has been rewarding — and what would it cost me to reverse the ones that matter most?
```

---

**How to use:**

The list needs to be specific and honest — this prompt does not work on generalities. "I've been more pragmatic" is not a list item. "I gave a 3 when I believed it was a 2, because the last time I gave a 2 I spent two months managing the fallout" is.

The AI cannot see your pattern. You have to surface it. What you get back is a mirror, not a verdict.

Ask the follow-up: *"Of these, which reversals would cost me the most professionally, and which would cost me most personally if I don't make them?"* The gap between those two lists is where the real decision lives.
