# Stress-Test Your Learning Stage

Use this when you want to find out how much of your current knowledge has actually been tested in practice — and what someone two stages ahead would challenge you on.

The six-stage model: Unaware → Aware → Understanding → Proficient → Mastery → Creative Mastery. Stage 2 (Aware) feels identical to Stage 4 (Proficient). This prompt makes the difference visible.

---

## The prompt

```
I've been working with [DOMAIN/SKILL] for [LENGTH OF TIME]. Here's what I currently do with it in practice: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF HOW YOU ACTUALLY USE IT — what you do day to day, not what you've read about].

I think I'm at Stage 2 or 3 of the learning model (Aware or Understanding — I know the concepts but I'm not sure how much of it is tested vs. theoretical).

What would a practitioner two stages ahead challenge me on specifically? What parts of my current understanding are probably theoretical rather than tested — and what would I actually have to do to test them?
```

---

**How to use:** Be honest in the description of how you actually use the skill — not what you've read or studied, but what you do with it in practice. The gap between what you write there and what the AI surfaces is the gap worth closing.

**What this reveals:** The difference between being familiar with a domain and being proficient in it. If the challenges feel abstract and distant, you're probably at Stage 2. If they sting a little, you're closer to Stage 3.
