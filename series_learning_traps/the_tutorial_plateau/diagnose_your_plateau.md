# Diagnose Your Plateau

Use this to find out where tutorial-based learning ends and real understanding begins. The prompt gives you a blank-canvas problem — no starter code, no hints, no structure — then debriefs honestly on what you actually understood vs. what the scaffold was providing.

The risk it addresses: the tutorial plateau. Completion feels like mastery. The Dunning-Kruger peak arrives early. This prompt moves the test from "did you follow the tutorial?" to "can you work without it?"

---

## The prompt

```
I have been learning [SKILL/TECHNOLOGY/DOMAIN] through tutorials. I believe I understand [LIST WHAT YOU THINK YOU KNOW — be specific, e.g. "component state management, routing, and API calls in React"].

Do not explain anything yet. Give me a blank-canvas problem that genuinely requires these skills — no starter code, no hints, no structure. I will attempt it and share my work.

Then tell me: what did I actually understand vs. what was I relying on the scaffold to provide?
```

---

**How to use:** Attempt the problem before coming back for the debrief. The gap between what you can produce from a blank file and what you thought you knew is the diagnosis. Share your actual attempt, not a description of what you would have done.

**What this reveals:** The specific line between knowledge you own and knowledge you were borrowing from the tutorial's structure. Scaffold dependency is invisible until the scaffold is gone.
