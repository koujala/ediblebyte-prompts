# The Passive Consumption Trap

Prompts from the **Passive Consumption Trap** article in the Learning Traps series.  
Read the full article: [ediblebyte.com/publications/series_learning_traps/the_passive_consumption_trap](https://www.ediblebyte.com/publications/series_learning_traps/the_passive_consumption_trap)

The article names the fluency illusion applied to high-volume consumption: familiarity with information produces a feeling of capability that the information does not create. Reading about courage is not practising courage. Recognising the right move in a book is not making the right move in a meeting with real stakes. And without application, 70% of new material is gone within 24 hours. The reading builds recognition. Recognition is not skill.

## Prompts

- [`convert_consumption_to_practice.md`](convert_consumption_to_practice.md) — Break the passive loop by applying a concept to a realistic scenario rather than receiving another explanation of it.
- [`close_the_loop_on_a_book.md`](close_the_loop_on_a_book.md) — Turn what you just finished reading into something you actually do within 48 hours.
