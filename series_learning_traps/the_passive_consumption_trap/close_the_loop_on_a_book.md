# Close the Loop on a Book or Course

Use this immediately after finishing a book, course, or extended piece of content — before the Forgetting Curve takes hold. The prompt converts the ideas you took away into specific actions you can take within 48 hours.

The risk it addresses: the passive consumption trap. Without application, 70% of new material is gone within 24 hours. This prompt is designed to close the gap between what you read and what you actually did.

---

## The prompt

```
I just finished [BOOK/COURSE] on [TOPIC]. The main things I took away were [LIST 2-3 IDEAS — brief, in your own words].

For each one, give me a specific, practical situation where I could apply it this week. Then check in: what is the smallest concrete action I could take with each idea in the next 48 hours?

I want to turn what I read into something I actually did.
```

---

**How to use:** Run this within a few hours of finishing — not a week later. The ideas are still accessible; the window is short. Keep the takeaways in your own words, not the book's framing. The 48-hour constraint is intentional — it forces specificity over ambition.

**What this reveals:** Whether the ideas you took away are actionable or aspirational. If you can't translate an idea into a 48-hour action, it hasn't landed yet — it's still a concept waiting for contact with reality.
