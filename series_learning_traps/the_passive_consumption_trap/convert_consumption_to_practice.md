# Convert Consumption to Practice

Use this when you feel you understand a concept from reading or watching — and want to move from recognition to application. The prompt refuses to re-explain the concept and instead puts it immediately into a realistic scenario.

The risk it addresses: the passive consumption trap. More content does not produce more capability. This prompt breaks the loop by shifting the interaction from delivery to performance.

---

## The prompt

```
I have been reading about [TOPIC] and I feel like I understand it conceptually. Do not explain it to me again.

Instead, give me a realistic scenario where I need to apply it — a situation where the concept is relevant but the solution is not obvious. Let me work through it. Then tell me where my application matched the theory and where it did not.
```

---

**How to use:** Resist the pull to add "can you first remind me of the key points?" — that's the passive loop reasserting itself. Go straight into the scenario. Work through it even if you're uncertain. The places where you hesitate are the places where the reading produced familiarity rather than understanding.

**What this reveals:** The gap between knowing what the right move looks like in a book and being able to produce it when the situation isn't labeled with the framework name. If you struggle to apply it to the scenario, more reading won't help — more practice will.
