# Test Under Pressure

Use this when you feel you understand something well enough — and want to find out whether that understanding holds when the conditions get uncomfortable. The prompt asks AI to simulate the real situation rather than re-explain the concept.

The risk it addresses: the finished feeling. Understanding survives a quiz; capability survives pressure. This prompt puts the understanding under pressure before the real moment does.

---

## The prompt

```
I understand [CONCEPT/SKILL] well enough to explain it — here's what I know: [BRIEF SUMMARY OF YOUR UNDERSTANDING IN YOUR OWN WORDS].

Now I need to test whether that understanding holds under pressure. Roleplay [THE SPECIFIC SITUATION — e.g. "a difficult performance conversation with a direct report who is defensive", or "a technical interview where the interviewer is pushing back on my approach"] and push back hard. Don't let me retreat to theory when the moment gets uncomfortable. Stop me when I'm describing what I'd do rather than actually doing it.

After we finish, tell me where my understanding was real and where it was still just a label.
```

---

**How to use:** Be specific about the situation you're simulating — generic roleplay produces generic results. The more accurately you describe the real context (the relationship, the stakes, the specific discomfort), the more useful the test will be.

**What this reveals:** The difference between understanding a framework and being able to execute it when the moment doesn't cooperate. If the understanding evaporates under mild pressure, the capability isn't there yet. The debrief at the end names exactly where the gap is.
