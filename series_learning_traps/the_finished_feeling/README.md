# The Finished Feeling

Prompts from the **Finished Feeling** article in the Learning Traps series.  
Read the full article: [ediblebyte.com/publications/series_learning_traps/the_finished_feeling](https://www.ediblebyte.com/publications/series_learning_traps/the_finished_feeling)

The article names the gap between understanding and capability — one of the most consistently underestimated distances in human development. Understanding is declarative; it lives in the part of the mind that recognises and recalls. Capability is procedural; it requires repeated, effortful, feedback-rich practice in conditions that resemble the real situation. The "finished feeling" — the satisfying click of having gotten something — signals the first, not the second.

- [`test_under_pressure.md`](test_under_pressure.md) — Move from declarative understanding to procedural capability by simulating the real conditions rather than discussing the concept.
