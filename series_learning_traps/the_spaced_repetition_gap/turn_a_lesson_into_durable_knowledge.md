# Turn a Lesson Into Durable Knowledge

Use this immediately after a learning session — a course lesson, a book chapter, a conversation, a workshop module. The prompt captures the three most important things to retain, builds retrieval questions for each, and sets a review structure before the Forgetting Curve starts.

The risk it addresses: the spaced repetition gap. The Forgetting Curve begins the moment learning stops. This prompt closes the window while the material is still accessible.

---

## The prompt

```
I just learned [DESCRIBE WHAT YOU LEARNED — from a course, a book, a conversation, a workshop]. I want to make sure I actually retain this rather than lose it to the Forgetting Curve.

Right now, before I forget, help me: identify the three most important things to retain, create retrieval questions for each one, and set a reminder structure for reviewing them at 24 hours, one week, and one month.

The questions should require me to reconstruct the idea, not just recognise it.
```

---

**How to use:** Run this within an hour of finishing the session — not the next day. The three-review structure (24 hours, one week, one month) maps to the known optimal intervals for moving information into long-term memory. Use it as actual calendar reminders. The retrieval questions are the mechanism — don't re-read the material at review time, answer the questions first.

**What this reveals:** Which parts of the session landed well enough to reconstruct from memory vs. which felt understood in the moment but have already started to fade. The 24-hour review is typically where the first real signal appears.
