# Premature Closure

Prompts from the **Premature Closure** article in the Learning Traps series.  
Read the full article: [ediblebyte.com/publications/series_learning_traps/premature_closure](https://www.ediblebyte.com/publications/series_learning_traps/premature_closure)

The article names cognitive closure — the mind's preference for certainty over ambiguity. A conclusion, even a premature one, releases the tension and allows the mind to move on. Most genuinely complex problems are still being understood at the point where the mind declares them solved. The cost is often invisible: not catastrophic failure, but adequate outcomes where deeper thinking was still available.

## Prompts

- [`challenge_before_you_act.md`](challenge_before_you_act.md) — Surface the fragile assumptions and skipped alternatives before committing to a conclusion.
