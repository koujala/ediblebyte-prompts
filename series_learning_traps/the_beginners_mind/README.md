# The Beginner's Mind Problem

Prompts from the **Beginner's Mind Problem** article in the Learning Traps series.  
Read the full article: [ediblebyte.com/publications/series_learning_traps/the_beginners_mind](https://www.ediblebyte.com/publications/series_learning_traps/the_beginners_mind)

The article names a problem nobody tells mid-career professionals: existing knowledge is not neutral — it actively blocks new learning. The more competent you are, the more your brain pattern-matches everything against what it already knows. In familiar territory, efficient. In new territory, dangerous.

The Zen concept of Shoshin (beginner's mind) addresses this. The practice isn't pretending you don't know what you know — it's suspending the assumption that what you know transfers.

- [`override_prior_assumptions.md`](override_prior_assumptions.md) — Activate beginner's mind by asking AI to explicitly catch and correct the assumptions your prior experience is creating.
