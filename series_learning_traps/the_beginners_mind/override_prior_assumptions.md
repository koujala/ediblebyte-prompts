# Override Prior Assumptions

Use this when learning something new where your existing expertise might be getting in the way. It operationalises beginner's mind — asking AI to actively catch and name the moments when your prior knowledge is distorting your understanding.

The risk it addresses: the more competent you are, the more your brain pattern-matches new information against what it already knows. Unfamiliar concepts feel wrong rather than just new. This prompt turns that into a named, visible event rather than a silent distortion.

---

## The prompt

```
I'm trying to learn [TOPIC/DOMAIN]. I'm coming from a background in [YOUR EXISTING FIELD OR EXPERTISE], which means I probably assume [COMMON ASSUMPTION YOUR BACKGROUND CREATES — e.g. "that state is always managed explicitly", or "that hierarchy matters more than consensus"].

Treat me as a complete beginner and override anything I seem to take for granted from my prior experience. When I get something wrong because my existing knowledge is shaping my thinking, name it explicitly — and show me why this domain works differently than I assumed.
```

---

**How to use:** The more specific you are about your background and the assumption it creates, the more useful the correction will be. Don't skip the third sentence — naming the assumption is what activates the override.

**What this reveals:** The specific places where your existing model is incompatible with the new domain, rather than just a general sense that "this is confusing." Named errors are fixable. Unnamed ones compound.
