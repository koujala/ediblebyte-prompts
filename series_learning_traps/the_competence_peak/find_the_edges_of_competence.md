# Find the Edges of Competence

Use this when you've been comfortable in your current work for a while and want to find out where demonstrated knowledge ends and assumed knowledge begins. The prompt uses progressively harder questions — without signaling when the edge is approaching — so you find it yourself.

The risk it addresses: the competence peak. Comfort removes the friction that signals falling behind. This prompt reintroduces that friction deliberately, in a low-stakes environment where the gap becoming visible is useful rather than costly.

---

## The prompt

```
I am a [ROLE/LEVEL — e.g. "mid-level software engineer", "senior product manager"] who has been comfortable in my current work for [TIME PERIOD — e.g. "about two years"]. I want to find the edges of what I actually know versus what I think I know.

Ask me progressively harder questions about [DOMAIN — e.g. "distributed systems", "product strategy", "financial modelling"] — start from solid fundamentals and move toward the frontier. Do not tell me when the questions start to exceed my demonstrated knowledge; let me find the edge myself.

Debrief at the end.
```

---

**How to use:** Answer the questions as you would in a real context — not as you would if you were studying for an exam. Don't look anything up mid-session. The questions that make you hesitate are the signal; don't skip past them. The debrief at the end is the payoff — it names the edge you found.

**What this reveals:** The line between what you actually know and what you've been assuming you know. The comfortable practitioner often discovers the edge is closer to the fundamentals than expected — or in areas they stopped questioning years ago.
