# Map What Is Next

Use this when you feel competent at your current level and want to understand what the next level actually requires — not in general terms, but specifically in contrast to what you can already do. The prompt returns concrete situations where the gap would be visible in practice.

The risk it addresses: the competence peak. The next level is hard to aim at when you can't see it clearly from where you're standing. This prompt makes the gap specific rather than aspirational.

---

## The prompt

```
I feel competent at [CURRENT LEVEL/SKILL — e.g. "writing clean component code in React", "running one-to-ones as a team lead"]. I want to understand what the next level actually requires — not in general terms, but specifically in contrast to what I can already do.

Here is what I can do well: [DESCRIBE — be concrete, e.g. "I can build well-structured components, manage local state cleanly, and debug rendering issues confidently"].

What are the capabilities I am not yet demonstrating? What does the gap look like in practice? Give me three concrete situations where the next level would produce a different outcome than I would currently produce.
```

---

**How to use:** Be honest and specific in the description of what you can do well — not humble and not inflated. The three situations at the end are the most useful output; they give you something to practise toward rather than just something to read about.

**What this reveals:** The specific capabilities that distinguish the next level from your current level, expressed as situations where the difference would be visible. Knowing the gap in abstract terms rarely changes behaviour; knowing it as three concrete situations gives you something to aim at.
