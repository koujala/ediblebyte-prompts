# Wall 4 Fix — Name Your Friction: Point the Basics Phase at What Actually Costs You Time

From Post 5 of the Your AI Path series: the corrective move for using AI opportunistically on random tasks rather than on real friction.

**The stall:** You use AI when someone mentions it can do something. You end up with a collection of one-off experiments that don't change how you work. The tool stays interesting but doesn't become useful.

**The problem:** Curiosity-driven experimentation doesn't build a habit — and a habit is what makes AI genuinely valuable. Friction is the specific, recurring tasks that cost you time and energy every single week. Those are the tasks to point the Basics Phase at.

## The fix prompt

Answer this before your next session:

```
What are the three things in my week that I consistently procrastinate on, rush, or wish I could skip?
```

Write down your actual answers. These are your friction points. Use the Basics Phase only on those for the next two weeks. Once those three are faster and less painful, expand.

## How to identify real friction

Real friction has these properties:
- It happens every week, not occasionally
- You dread it, rush it, or do it badly because of time pressure
- If it disappeared, you'd notice immediately

Random tasks that "seem like something AI could help with" are not friction. They're curiosity. Both are fine — but only friction builds the habit.

## The rule

Name your actual friction points before every session. Point the work at those specifically. Random experimentation doesn't build the habit that makes AI valuable.
