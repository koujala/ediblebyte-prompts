# Wall 2 Fix — Iterate, Don't Accept: Reply with What's Off

From Post 5 of the Your AI Path series: the corrective move for accepting the first output and closing the tab.

**The stall:** You get a response. It's pretty good. You copy it and move on. You've used AI as a slightly smarter template engine — not as a tool.

**The problem:** The first output is always a draft. The model doesn't know your specific preferences, your organisation's tone, or the context that makes this situation different from the average case. The real value comes from iteration.

## The fix prompts

After the first response, reply in the same chat. Choose the instruction that matches what's wrong:

**When the output is too generic:**
```
The second section is too generic — it reads like advice for anyone, not for my specific situation. Make it more direct and cut the filler. Also, the tone is too formal — we're a startup, not a law firm.
```

**When the structure is fine but the opening is weak:**
```
Good structure. The opening is weak though — I need it to land harder. Try three different versions of just the opening paragraph.
```

**When you want to narrow in on one part:**
```
Ignore everything except [specific section]. Rewrite just that section with [specific instruction — e.g., sharper language / more concrete examples / half the word count].
```

**The simplest fix of all:**
```
That's not quite right. Here's what's wrong with it: [explain]. Try again.
```

## The rule

Draft one is always a starting point. Reply with what's off. Go two or three rounds before deciding whether the output is useful. The gap between people who use AI well and people who don't is often just one more reply.
