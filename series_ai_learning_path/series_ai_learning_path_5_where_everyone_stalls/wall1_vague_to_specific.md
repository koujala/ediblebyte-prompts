# Wall 1 Fix — Vague to Specific: Give the Model Something to Work With

From Post 5 of the Your AI Path series: the corrective move for the most common beginner mistake — treating the model like a search engine.

**The stall:** You type a vague question. You get a generic list. You feel mildly disappointed and assume the tool is overrated.

**The problem:** A search engine returns documents from the web — specificity doesn't help much. A language model generates based on what you give it. The output matches the depth of the input.

## The before (what stalls you)

```
How do I write a good performance review?
```

Generic input. Generic output. Technically correct. Useful to nobody.

## The fix

```
I'm a manager at a 50-person tech startup. I need to write a performance review for a software engineer who has been strong technically but has struggled with communication in cross-functional meetings — specifically, they don't raise blockers until the last minute and they're sometimes dismissive of non-technical stakeholders.

I want to be honest, constructive, and specific. Help me structure the review and draft the key sections.
```

## What changed

The fixed version gives the model: role, company context, specific strengths, specific development areas, and the tone you're aiming for. It has something to work with.

## The rule

Give the model what you would give a new hire on their first day. At minimum:
- Your role and relevant context
- What you're trying to achieve
- Who the audience is
- Any constraints (tone, length, format, what to avoid)

Specificity in — specificity out.
