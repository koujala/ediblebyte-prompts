# Finance Professional — CFO Board Narrative

From Post 2 of the Your AI Path series: the example prompt showing what Phase 1 looks like for a finance professional.

The problem this solves: financial insight requires interpretation — the skill that makes a finance professional valuable. But interpretation only shows up after the data has been packaged into a narrative, which takes hours. This prompt produces the first-draft narrative around your numbers in minutes; the expertise goes into validating, sharpening, and presenting.

## The prompt

```
Here is a summary of our Q3 financial results: revenue up 12% YoY, gross margin down 3 points, operating expenses up 18%, net income flat.

Write a two-page CFO narrative for the board that explains what drove each of these movements, what it means for Q4, and what three decisions the board should be considering.
```

## How to use

Provide your actual numbers — revenue, margin, expenses, net income, and any key variances you want explained. The more specific your data, the more specific the narrative. Specify the audience (board, investors, leadership team) and tone — direct and confident with financial literacy assumed, or simplified for a mixed audience.

The model will generate plausible explanations for the movements based on what you've told it. You validate those explanations against the actual business context — the model can't know what drove the numbers, only you can.
