# HR — Job Description

From Post 2 of the Your AI Path series: the example prompt showing what Phase 1 looks like for an HR professional.

The problem this solves: HR is one of the most documentation-heavy functions in any organisation. Job descriptions alone require precise language and consistent tone. This prompt reduces a 90-minute drafting task to 5 minutes of reviewing and adjusting.

## The prompt

```
Write a job description for a Senior Product Manager at a mid-stage B2B SaaS company.

The role is remote, focused on our enterprise product line. Requirements: 5+ years product experience, strong data skills, experience with enterprise customers.

Tone should be direct and specific — no generic filler language.

Include responsibilities, requirements, and a short 'why us' section.
```

## How to use

Replace the job title, company stage, industry, location/remote setup, and requirements with your actual role details. Specify tone explicitly — "direct and no-filler" produces a different output to "warm and culture-forward." Ask it to avoid specific phrases you hate in JDs (e.g., "fast-paced environment," "wear many hats") to get cleaner output.

The first draft will need adjusting for company-specific specifics the model can't know. That adjustment is where the time savings are most visible — editing a draft takes a fraction of writing from scratch.
