# Student — Study Guide and Self-Quiz from Lecture Notes

From Post 2 of the Your AI Path series: the example prompt showing what Phase 1 looks like for a student using AI as a thinking partner rather than a shortcut.

The problem this solves: passive re-reading that doesn't actually build understanding. This prompt turns the model into a Socratic tutor available at 2am — infinitely patient, able to rephrase, and able to give feedback on your reasoning, not just your answers.

## The prompt

```
I'm studying for an exam on Keynesian economics. Here are my lecture notes:

[paste notes]

Create a study guide that explains the key concepts in plain language, then give me 10 exam-style questions to test myself. After I answer, tell me where my reasoning is off.
```

## How to use

Paste your actual lecture notes — not a cleaned-up version, but whatever you have. The model works with raw material. Specify the subject, the specific topic, and what exam you're preparing for (open-book, essay, multiple choice) so the questions match the format.

After it gives you the questions, answer them one at a time in the same chat. Ask it to tell you not just whether you were right, but where your reasoning went wrong. That's where the value is.
