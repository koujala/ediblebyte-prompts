# Creative — Campaign Concept Directions

From Post 2 of the Your AI Path series: the example prompt showing what Phase 1 looks like for a creative.

The problem this solves: the blank page problem. The difficulty isn't a lack of creativity — it's starting. Once there's something to react to, creative judgment activates. This prompt produces 8 directions in 60 seconds; the creative rejects most of them and builds from the ones that don't feel wrong.

## The prompt

```
I'm creative director for a campaign for a sustainable running shoe brand targeting urban millennials.

The brief is: 'Run your city without leaving a mark.'

Give me 8 creative directions — different tones, angles, and visual territories — that I can react to and build from. Include a headline, one-line visual concept, and the emotional idea for each.
```

## How to use

Replace the brand, audience, and brief with your own. Be specific about the brief — a loose brief produces loose directions. Specify tone ranges if there are directions you want to avoid (e.g., "nothing ironic," "avoid corporate polish"). Ask for variety across approaches: some literal, some abstract, some provocative, some warm.

The value is in having 8 things to react to, not 8 things to approve. Reject freely. The two or three that feel interesting are the real output.
