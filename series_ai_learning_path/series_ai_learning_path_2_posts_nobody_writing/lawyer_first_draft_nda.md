# Lawyer — First-Pass Contract Draft

From Post 2 of the Your AI Path series: the example prompt showing what Phase 1 looks like for a lawyer.

The problem this solves: first-pass drafting is billable but not particularly valuable — it's a starting-point task that doesn't require the expertise the client is paying for. The blank page still slows things down. This prompt produces a first draft to review in 20 minutes instead of writing in 90.

**Note:** AI-generated legal drafts require professional review. The value is eliminating the blank page — the expertise goes into editing, flagging jurisdiction-specific risks, and advising.

## The prompt

```
Draft a non-disclosure agreement between a UK-based technology startup (disclosing party) and a potential enterprise client (receiving party).

Mutual obligations. Two-year term. Covers: product roadmap, pricing, technical architecture, and customer data.

Include standard carve-outs for public information and prior knowledge.
```

## How to use

Replace the parties, jurisdiction, term, and scope with your actual matter. Be specific about what information is being protected and what the carve-outs should cover. Ask it to flag clauses that typically require jurisdiction-specific review — this surfaces where your professional judgment needs to go.

The output is a working draft, not a finished document. Treat it as you would a junior associate's first pass.
