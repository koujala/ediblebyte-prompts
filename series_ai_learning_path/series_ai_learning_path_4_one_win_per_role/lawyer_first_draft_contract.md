# Lawyer — First-Pass Contract Draft

The highest-impact Basics Phase move for a lawyer: a first-pass draft to work from instead of a blank page.

**What changes:** First-draft time drops from 90 minutes to 15 minutes of reviewing and refining.

**Note:** AI-generated legal drafts require professional review. The prompt removes the blank page — the expertise goes into editing, flagging risks, and advising.

## The prompt

```
Draft a [agreement type — e.g., NDA / service agreement / consulting contract] between:
- Party A: [describe — company type, jurisdiction]
- Party B: [describe — company type, jurisdiction]

Key terms:
- Duration: [timeframe]
- Scope: [what is being protected/agreed to]
- Key obligations: [list 3-5]
- Governing law: [jurisdiction]

Include standard carve-outs for [publicly known information / prior knowledge / independently developed materials].
Flag any clauses where you'd typically recommend jurisdiction-specific legal review.
```

## How to use

Fill in the agreement type, parties, key terms, and jurisdiction. Be specific about scope — what exactly is being protected or agreed to. The more specific the key obligations, the more useful the draft.

The instruction to flag clauses needing jurisdiction-specific review is important: it surfaces where your professional judgment needs to go and prompts the model to be explicit about the limits of a generic first draft.

Treat the output as a junior associate's first pass — useful structure, requires your expertise to finalise.
