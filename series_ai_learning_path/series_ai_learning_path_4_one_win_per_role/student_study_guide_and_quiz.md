# Student — Study Guide and Self-Quiz from Lecture Notes

The highest-impact Basics Phase move for a student: a study guide and self-quiz from your own notes — not someone else's interpretation of the topic.

**What changes:** Passive re-reading becomes active, testable understanding. The model becomes a tutor at 2am.

## The prompt

```
Here are my lecture notes from today's [subject] class on [topic]:
[paste notes]

Do the following:
1. Rewrite the key concepts in plain language — assume I understand the basics but struggle with [specific area]
2. Create a study guide that structures the main ideas clearly
3. Write 10 exam-style questions that test understanding, not just recall
4. After I answer, tell me what I got right, what I missed, and where my reasoning is off

Ask me the questions one at a time. Wait for my answer before moving on.
```

## How to use

Paste your actual lecture notes — unedited, exactly as you captured them. The model works from raw material. Fill in `[specific area]` with whatever concept you're least confident about; this focuses the plain-language rewrite where it's most useful.

After it gives you the questions, answer them one at a time in the same chat session. Don't move on until you've answered. The feedback on your reasoning — not just whether you were right — is where the value is.

The students who benefit from this prompt use it to think harder. The ones who don't copy the study guide and close the tab. The prompt only works if you answer the questions.
