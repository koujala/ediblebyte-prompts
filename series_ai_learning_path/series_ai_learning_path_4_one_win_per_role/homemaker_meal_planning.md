# Homemaker — Weekly Meal Plan from Existing Ingredients

The highest-impact Basics Phase move for a homemaker: a full week of meals from what's already in the house — no extra shopping trip.

**What changes:** No more 6pm "what's for dinner" panic. No wasted groceries at the end of the week.

## The prompt

```
I have the following ingredients at home: [list everything in your fridge and pantry].
My family: [number of people, any dietary restrictions or preferences].
I want to avoid food waste this week.

Give me:
- 5 dinners using only these ingredients
- 3 lunches
- One batch-cooking idea for the weekend that uses up anything I haven't touched

For each meal, give me the steps in plain language. Nothing that takes more than 45 minutes on a weeknight.
```

## How to use

List everything you actually have — the more complete the list, the better the suggestions. Be specific about restrictions (allergies, dietary preferences, age of children) since these meaningfully change the output.

If a suggested meal doesn't work, reply with "swap that one — what else uses [ingredient]?" Iterate until you have five dinners you'd actually cook.

After a few weeks, you'll notice the prompt getting more efficient as you build up a sense of how to phrase your pantry list for the best results.
