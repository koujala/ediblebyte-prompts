# Project Manager — Meeting Notes to Stakeholder Update

The highest-impact Basics Phase move for a project manager: raw meeting notes turned into a polished stakeholder update — ready to send.

**What changes:** 45 minutes of post-meeting admin becomes 5 minutes of review.

## The prompt

```
Here are the raw notes from our [project name] sync meeting today:
[paste notes]

From these, give me:
1. A bullet list of decisions made (with who made them)
2. Action items — who owns each, and by when
3. Blockers or risks that need escalation — with a suggested next step for each
4. A two-paragraph email update I can send to senior stakeholders who weren't in the meeting

Tone: direct, no jargon, assume the reader has high context on the project but limited time.
```

## How to use

Paste the raw notes — unedited, exactly as captured in the room. The model handles the structure. Replace `[project name]` with your actual project so the output uses the right framing in the stakeholder email.

If the stakeholder email tone is wrong, reply with a single instruction: "make it shorter" or "the second paragraph is too vague — be more specific about the Q2 blocker." Go one round of iteration before deciding whether the output is ready to send.

Run this immediately after every sync meeting — the habit compounds. When stakeholders consistently get clear, timely updates, you spend less time in follow-up conversations and more time on the work that matters.
