# Finance Professional — CFO Board Narrative

The highest-impact Basics Phase move for a finance professional: the board narrative written around your numbers — not starting from scratch.

**What changes:** Report narrative time drops from half a day to an hour of editing.

## The prompt

```
Here is a summary of our [Q/year] financial results:
[paste your data — revenue, margin, expenses, net income, key variances]

Write a two-page CFO narrative for a board audience. Include:
1. What drove the key movements (not just what moved — why)
2. What this means for [next quarter/next year]
3. Three questions or decisions the board should be focused on given these results
4. One risk and one opportunity that the numbers surface

Tone: direct, confident, assumes financial literacy. No filler language.
```

## How to use

Paste your actual numbers — revenue, margin, operating expenses, net income, and any key variances you want explained. Replace `[Q/year]` with your reporting period and `[next quarter/next year]` with the forward-looking period relevant to your board.

The model will generate plausible narrative explanations based on what you've told it. You validate those explanations against the actual business context — it cannot know what drove the numbers, only you can. Edit the "why" sections to reflect reality before presenting.

The three board questions and the risk/opportunity sections are often the most useful starting points — they surface framing you may want to challenge or refine.
