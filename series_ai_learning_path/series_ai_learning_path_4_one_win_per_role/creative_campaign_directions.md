# Creative — Campaign Concept Directions

The highest-impact Basics Phase move for a creative: 10 concept directions from a brief — to react to, not to start from.

**What changes:** The first 30 minutes of a creative brief stop being the hardest 30 minutes.

## The prompt

```
I'm working on a creative campaign for [brand/product].
Brief: [one paragraph — the brand, the audience, the feeling, the message]
Constraint: [any mandatory elements — format, tone, restrictions]

Give me 10 creative directions. For each one, give me:
- A working title
- A one-line concept (the idea in plain language)
- A headline
- A visual territory (what it looks and feels like — describe it)
- The emotional idea (what the audience should feel)

Vary the approaches — some literal, some abstract, some provocative, some warm.
I want to react to these, not approve them. Be bold.
```

## How to use

Write a specific brief — one tight paragraph covering the brand, the audience, the feeling you're after, and the core message. A loose brief produces loose directions. Fill in `[Constraint]` with anything that's mandatory or off-limits (format requirements, tone guardrails, regulatory restrictions).

The instruction "I want to react to these, not approve them" is not decorative — it signals to the model to push further than it might otherwise go. Expect to reject most of the 10. The two or three that feel interesting are the actual output.

If nothing lands, reply with "none of these are right — the [literal / abstract / provocative] directions are closest, try five more in that territory." Don't accept the first 10 as the only options.
