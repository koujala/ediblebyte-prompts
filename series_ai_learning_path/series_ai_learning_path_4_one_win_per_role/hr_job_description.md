# HR — Job Description

The highest-impact Basics Phase move for an HR professional: a complete, specific job description in two minutes instead of 90.

**What changes:** JD drafting goes from 90 minutes to 10 minutes of reviewing and customising.

## The prompt

```
Write a job description for a [job title] at a [company size/stage] [industry] company.

Details:
- Location/remote: [specifics]
- Team context: [what team, what they work on]
- Core responsibilities: [list 4-5 key areas]
- Must-have requirements: [list 3-5]
- Nice-to-have: [list 2-3]
- Tone: [direct and no-filler / friendly and culture-forward / formal and structured]

Avoid generic language like "fast-paced environment" or "wear many hats." Be specific about what this person will actually do in their first 90 days.
Include a short "why join us" section — not about perks, about the work itself.
```

## How to use

Fill in every field — the specificity drives the output quality. The `[Tone]` field matters: "direct and no-filler" produces a fundamentally different document to "warm and culture-forward."

The first-90-days instruction is important — it forces specificity that generic JDs avoid. Review that section carefully; it's the part the model is most likely to generate plausibly but inaccurately.

Adjust the "why join us" section last, after the rest is right. That section reflects your company's actual situation; the model can only approximate it.
