# Sales Pro — Pre-Call Preparation

The highest-impact Basics Phase move for a sales professional: walk into every call more prepared than the competition — in under 5 minutes.

**What changes:** Every call starts from research, not guesswork.

## The prompt

```
I'm meeting with [name], [job title] at [company] tomorrow.
Here's their LinkedIn profile: [paste bio].
Here's their company's about page: [paste or summarise].
I sell [your product/service — one sentence on what it does and who it's for].

Give me:
1. Three likely pain points based on their role and company context
2. Two opening questions that show I've done my homework
3. The two objections I'm most likely to hear and how to handle them
4. One insight or angle that connects their background to what I offer
```

## How to use

Paste the actual LinkedIn bio and company about page — don't summarise them yourself first. The model extracts signal from the specific language people use to describe their roles and companies. Replace `[your product/service]` with one clear sentence: what it does and who it's for.

Use the pain points and objections as hypotheses to test in the conversation, not assertions to lead with. The opening questions are most effective when they're genuinely curious rather than used to signal preparation.

Run this before every call, not just the big ones. The cumulative advantage of consistent prep compounds over time.
