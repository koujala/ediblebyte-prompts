# Self-Deception Series — Prompts

Companion prompt library for the **Self-Deception Series** on [ediblebyte.com](https://www.ediblebyte.com/publications/series_self_deception).

Each post in the series exposes a different way the mind deceives itself — overthinking dressed as diligence, confidence that outpaces competence, the validation loop that keeps conclusions comfortable. These prompts let you use AI to surface and interrupt those patterns in your own thinking.

## How to use these prompts

Every prompt file is self-contained. Copy its contents into Claude, ChatGPT, or any LLM — fill in the bracketed `[PLACEHOLDERS]` with your specifics, and read the response honestly rather than for confirmation.

The prompts work best when you are in the pattern, not after. Use them in the moment — when you are about to make a decision, when you notice the stall, when you catch yourself hunting for agreement.

## The underlying discipline

The series runs on one observation: the mind is not a neutral instrument. It is a motivated reasoner with a preference for comfort, certainty, and an unthreatened self-image. AI is useful here not because it is smarter, but because it has no stake in the outcome. It will evaluate the situation on what is described, without softening for the relationship or factoring in the good intention behind the behaviour.

That makes it an unusually honest mirror — if you ask it the right question.
