# Weekly Output Audit

Use this prompt at the end of the week to get an honest read on whether your activity produced progress or just produced activity. AI has no interest in your effort level — only in what moved.

The article identifies the mechanism: institutions reward visible effort because visible effort is easier to observe than outcomes. That reward gets internalised. This prompt uses AI's indifference to effort to break the accounting.

---

## Prompt

```
Here is what I worked on this week: [DESCRIBE YOUR WEEK — meetings attended, tasks completed, conversations had, work produced].

Here are the goals I said mattered this month: [LIST THEM — be specific about what progress on each goal looks like].

Tell me honestly: how much of what I did this week moved those goals forward? What was necessary support work, what was genuine progress, and what was activity that did not serve either? I want the honest version, not the encouraging one.
```

---

**How to use:** Write the week description as you would naturally describe it — including all the things you did. Do not pre-edit out the meetings or the email. Then read the response against the discomfort rather than through it.

**When to use it:** End of week, before planning the next one. The gap between what you expected to see and what the audit shows is information.
