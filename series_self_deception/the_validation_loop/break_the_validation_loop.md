# Break the Validation Loop

Use this prompt after you have consulted several people and found agreement — specifically when that agreement came after a series of disagreements. The article names this jury shopping: the search ends when a preferred answer is found, not when the question is answered.

The article notes that AI is not immune to this — feed it a leading question and it will lean toward the implied answer. The prompt below is designed to counteract that by explicitly asking for the fundamental disagreement, not the surface objection.

---

## Prompt

```
I've consulted several people on [DECISION / DIRECTION / IDEA] and the consensus I've landed on is: [STATE THE POSITION].

Now tell me the strongest case against it — not a surface objection or a minor caveat, but a fundamental one that someone who genuinely disagrees would make. What does the most credible dissent look like? What is the concern that the people who agreed with me most likely glossed over?
```

---

**How to use:** State the consensus position accurately — including the reasoning behind it, not just the conclusion. The prompt is asking for the strongest version of the case against that reasoning.

**What to watch for:** The article describes the validation loop as ending when one agreeing voice closes the loop — the three dissenting voices received no additional weight because there were three of them. If the response surfaces something that resembles what the dissenters said, that is the loop operating as described. The certainty purchased through a validation loop is doubt that has been successfully avoided, not certainty that has been earned.
