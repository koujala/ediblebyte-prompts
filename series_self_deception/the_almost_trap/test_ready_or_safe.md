# Test Whether It Is Ready or Just Safe

Use this prompt when you have been working on something for an extended period and keep finding reasons it is not ready to share. AI evaluates the output against the standard you define — not against your anxiety about what happens if it ships.

The article frames this as the core test: preparation gets you closer to a concrete outcome; protection delays being seen. First attempts are research, not performances.

---

## Prompt

```
I have been working on [PROJECT / IDEA / PIECE OF WORK] for [TIME PERIOD] and I keep finding reasons it is not ready to share.

Here is what I have: [DESCRIBE WHAT EXISTS NOW].

Here is the standard it needs to meet: [DESCRIBE WHAT GOOD LOOKS LIKE — the audience, the purpose, the minimum bar for usefulness].

Evaluate it against that standard honestly. Is this genuinely not ready, or is it ready enough to ship and learn from? What specifically is missing if it is not, and what is the smallest version that would be ready?
```

---

**How to use:** Be precise about the standard — not "I want it to be excellent" but what the work actually needs to do and for whom. The more concrete the standard, the less room there is for "not yet" to operate.

**What to watch for:** If the response says it is ready and your first instinct is to explain why that assessment is wrong, notice that. The article calls that instinct the almost trap in operation.
