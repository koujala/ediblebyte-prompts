# Identify the Real Threshold

Use this prompt when you have been "almost ready" to take a specific step for longer than makes sense. It asks AI to examine each blocker and determine whether it is a genuine quality gap or a mechanism for avoiding the moment of being seen.

The article names this as the Planning Fallacy in operation: we consistently underestimate how long "almost ready" will take and overestimate how much one more preparation will reduce the risk.

---

## Prompt

```
I have been "almost ready" to [LAUNCH / PUBLISH / APPLY / PROPOSE / START] for [TIME PERIOD].

Here is what I keep saying I need first: [LIST THE CURRENT BLOCKERS — each condition or preparation step that feels required before you can proceed].

For each blocker, help me determine: is this a genuine quality gap, or is this a protection mechanism? What would I need to believe about myself or the outcome for this to feel ready? Am I solving the preparation problem or the fear problem?
```

---

**How to use:** List your actual blockers honestly — the things you tell yourself and others. The response will examine each one. Some will be real; some will not. Both are useful to see.

**What to watch for:** Blockers that are vague ("I need more confidence," "I need it to be better") are almost always protection. Blockers that are specific ("the pricing section is incomplete") are usually preparation. The prompt helps sort them.
