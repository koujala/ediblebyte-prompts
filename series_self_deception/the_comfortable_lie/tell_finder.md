# The Tell-Finder

Use this prompt to examine the sequence of a decision and identify where the conclusion may have preceded the analysis. The article's term: the alibi is constructed to survive scrutiny, not to surface truth. This prompt looks for the construction sequence.

---

## Prompt

```
Here's how I made this decision: [DESCRIBE THE SEQUENCE — when you first formed an opinion about the direction, when you gathered information, what you did with it, which alternatives you pursued and which you dropped, and when the conclusion felt settled].

Looking at this sequence honestly, where does it look like my conclusion preceded my analysis? I'm not looking to defend the decision. I'm looking to identify the rationalisation.
```

---

**How to use:** Write the sequence as it actually happened, not as it would look in a retrospective presentation. Include the moments of doubt, the alternatives you considered briefly and then put down, the point where the search felt complete.

**What to watch for:** The article identifies the tell as the moment when inconvenient evidence got weighted lightly and confirming evidence got weighted heavily. In the sequence, this often shows up as: gathering information until one source agreed with the instinct, then stopping. The prompt is asking AI to spot that pattern in the description you provide.
