# The Scratch Test

Use this prompt to test whether your current direction would survive a fresh-eyes assessment — one with no investment in the work already done, no sunk cost, no emotional attachment to the existing path.

The article names this the alibi: the analysis looks rigorous because it uses rigorous language, but the conclusion was predetermined. The gap between what AI recommends from scratch and what you are doing is where the comfortable lie lives.

---

## Prompt

```
Here's my situation: [DESCRIBE YOUR CONTEXT — the decision made, the direction being pursued, what has been committed to and why].

Ignore everything I've already committed to. If you were advising someone starting fresh today — no sunk costs, no prior commitments, just the facts of the situation — what would you recommend?

How does that compare to what I'm currently doing?
```

---

**How to use:** Describe the situation factually, including the constraints. Do not frame it in a way that leads to the conclusion you want — the prompt only works if the description is honest.

**What to watch for:** The gap between the fresh recommendation and your current path. A small gap may mean your direction is sound. A large gap is worth examining — not to reverse the decision automatically, but to understand whether you can articulate a genuine reason for the difference, or whether the difference is the rationalisation.
