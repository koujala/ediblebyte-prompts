# Steelman the Case Against Yourself

Use this prompt when you hold a strong position on a situation and want to test whether you are searching for truth or searching for confirmation of what you already believe.

The article's diagnostic: if the case against your position can be dismissed without discomfort, it probably wasn't examined. The goal is not comfort — it is the version of the challenge that requires you to actually sit with it.

---

## Prompt

```
Here is my position on [SITUATION / DECISION / BELIEF]: [STATE YOUR POSITION CLEARLY].

Build the strongest possible case that I'm simply wrong — not partially right with caveats, just wrong. Make it as uncomfortable as possible. Don't soften it or balance it with what I might have right. I want the strongest version of the disagreement.
```

---

**How to use:** State your position as clearly and confidently as you actually hold it. The stronger the position going in, the more useful the steelman. Do not pre-hedge by saying "I might be wrong about this" — state what you actually believe.

**The test the article describes:** Sit with the response long enough to evaluate it honestly rather than immediately. If your first instinct is to find a counter-argument, notice whether you are engaging with the argument or deflecting it. The article's question: are you looking for whether you were right, or are you actually looking for what is true? They produce different searches.
