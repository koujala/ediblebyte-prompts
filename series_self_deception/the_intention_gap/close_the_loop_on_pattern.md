# Close the Loop on a Recurring Pattern

Use this prompt when you have a recurring behaviour that creates a gap between your intention and how others experience you — and you want to understand what the pattern looks like from the other side before it costs you.

The article identifies the gap as invisible to us and visible to everyone around us. We are the last person to close it precisely because we are the last person to see it.

---

## Prompt

```
I have a pattern of [DESCRIBE THE RECURRING BEHAVIOUR — slow responses, vague feedback, avoiding hard conversations, dominating discussions, over-committing and under-delivering, etc.].

I tell myself it is because [YOUR INTERNAL REASONING — deep work, thoughtfulness, not wanting to be harsh, etc.].

Help me see what this looks like from the other side — specifically for [THE PERSON OR TYPE OF PERSON MOST AFFECTED BY THIS PATTERN]. What are they experiencing? What conclusions are they drawing about me? What would I need to change for the behaviour to match the intention?
```

---

**How to use:** Name the pattern honestly — not the polished version of it. The internal reasoning section is where most people soften the description; resist that. The response is only as useful as the description is accurate.

**The follow-up question the article suggests:** "Did that land the way I intended?" — a five-second question that provides information no internal self-assessment can give you. This prompt prepares the ground for asking it.
