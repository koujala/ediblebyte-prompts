# Read Your Own Behaviour from the Outside

Use this prompt when you want to understand how a specific action or communication landed with others — without the benefit of your good intention softening the read. AI evaluates behaviour as behaviour, not behaviour plus stated intention.

The article frames this as: AI has no interest in the more flattering version. It doesn't factor in the relationship, doesn't soften for social courtesy, doesn't weight the intention alongside the act. For people consistently surprised by how they land with others, this is one of the fastest calibration tools available.

---

## Prompt

```
Here is something I did or said recently: [DESCRIBE THE BEHAVIOUR — what you did, what you said, in what context, to whom].

Here is what I intended: [EXPLAIN YOUR INTENTION — what you were trying to achieve or communicate].

Tell me how this behaviour reads from the outside, without my intention in the frame. What would someone observing only the behaviour conclude? Where is the gap between what I meant and what I did?
```

---

**How to use:** Describe the behaviour as specifically as possible — the actual words, the timing, the context. Vague descriptions produce vague assessments. The response is most useful when it is uncomfortable, not when it confirms the intention was good.

**What to do with it:** The article's test: after your next interaction, ask not what you intended, but what they experienced. This prompt prepares you to ask that question honestly.
