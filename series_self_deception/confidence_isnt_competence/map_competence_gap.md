# Map the Competence Gap

Use this prompt when you want to understand the difference between sounding competent at your current level and genuinely being competent — specifically, what the next stage of development actually requires.

The dangerous phase of learning is when you have the vocabulary and the frameworks but not yet the judgment. This prompt names that gap concretely so you can see what you are still building.

---

## Prompt

```
I've been working in [DOMAIN / FIELD / ROLE] for [X YEARS / TIME PERIOD].

Describe the difference between someone who sounds competent at this level and someone who genuinely is. What do they do differently when it gets hard?

Specifically: what does the next stage of competence actually require — not the vocabulary, not the frameworks, but the judgment? And what kinds of mistakes does someone at my current level typically make without realising it?
```

---

**How to use:** Fill in your domain and honest time in the field. Read the response without defensiveness — the mistakes section is the useful part. The goal is not to feel assessed; it is to see what you do not yet see.

**What to watch for:** If the response describes things you already feel confident about, that may be evidence of the gap the article names. Genuine competence tends to produce more uncertainty, not less.
