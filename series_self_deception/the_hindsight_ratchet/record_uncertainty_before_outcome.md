# Record the Uncertainty Before the Outcome

Use this prompt before a decision plays out — to create a timestamped record of your actual uncertainty that memory cannot revise later. The article's point: if you did not write it down, you are remembering a story, not a decision.

AI conversations are not reconstructive. The record exists independently of what you remember later. The gap between what you wrote and what you remember is the hindsight bias in operation.

---

## Prompt

```
I am about to make a decision about [DESCRIBE THE DECISION].

Before I know the outcome, I want to record my actual state of mind. Ask me:
- What are the two or three most plausible outcomes?
- How confident am I in each one, as a rough percentage?
- What is the key uncertainty that will determine which way it goes?
- What would I conclude about my judgment if it goes well? If it goes badly?

I want this on record so I cannot revise it later.
```

---

**How to use:** Run this prompt and answer each question honestly before the outcome is known. Save the conversation or copy the responses somewhere you will find later. When the outcome arrives, compare what you wrote to what you remember having felt.

**The key question to revisit:** After the outcome, how confident do you now remember being? The article identifies this as the ratchet — good outcomes get absorbed as evidence of foresight; the uncertainty recorded here is the actual evidence.
