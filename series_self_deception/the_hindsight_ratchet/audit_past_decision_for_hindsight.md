# Audit a Past Decision for Hindsight Bias

Use this prompt to examine a past decision and find the gap between what you actually knew at the time and what you now believe you knew. The article identifies the tell: the clarity you remember having is the clarity you invented afterward.

---

## Prompt

```
I made a decision about [DESCRIBE THE DECISION] and it [WENT WELL / WENT BADLY].

I remember feeling [CONFIDENT / CERTAIN / CLEAR / UNCERTAIN] about it at the time.

Help me examine whether that memory is accurate or whether I am applying hindsight. Ask me:
- What did I actually know when I made the decision?
- What was genuinely uncertain?
- What alternatives did I seriously consider?
- What would I have said the probability of success was before the outcome?

I want to find out what I actually knew versus what I now believe I knew.
```

---

**How to use:** This prompt works best when you answer the follow-up questions honestly rather than immediately. The goal is not to evaluate whether the decision was good — it is to calibrate the model of your own judgment that you carry forward.

**What to watch for:** The article describes a completely distorted model of decision quality that improves only the story, never the process. If a good outcome "proves" your judgment, and a bad outcome "proves" circumstances beyond your control, neither experience is teaching you anything. This prompt is designed to break that accounting.
