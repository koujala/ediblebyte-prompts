# Build the Right Reference Class

Use this prompt to replace the single visible outlier you are anchored to with a realistic picture of outcomes for people in your actual position. The article's point: the wrong reference class produces wrong conclusions, and wrong conclusions produce wrong decisions.

AI cannot give you perfect data, but it can replace one anecdote with a more honest range.

---

## Prompt

```
I want to recalibrate my sense of where I should be at this point in my career.

Here is my situation: [DESCRIBE YOUR BACKGROUND, FIELD, TIMELINE, CONSTRAINTS, AND STARTING POINT — include the factors that shaped your path, not just the current role].

Rather than comparing to the most successful person I know, help me think about a realistic reference class: what do outcomes typically look like for people with similar starting conditions? What does genuine progress look like at my stage, rather than exceptional progress at someone else's?
```

---

**How to use:** Include the starting conditions honestly — not just where you are now, but where you started, what constraints you navigated, what timing you caught or missed. The reference class only works if it is calibrated to your actual trajectory, not an idealised version of it.

**The question that matters:** The article frames this as: am I moving? Not: am I where someone else is? This prompt is designed to help you answer the first question correctly.
