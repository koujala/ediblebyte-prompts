# Audit Your Comparison Anchor

Use this prompt to examine the person or outcome you consistently measure yourself against. The article frames this as measuring a marathon by the position of someone who started three miles ahead — and calling it a performance gap.

AI can surface the likely invisible inputs: the advantages, timing, inherited starting conditions, and structural luck that do not appear in the visible outcome but are part of the actual equation.

---

## Prompt

```
I regularly compare myself to [DESCRIBE THE PERSON OR OUTCOME YOU ANCHOR TO — their role, career stage, visible success, or specific achievement].

When I do this comparison, I feel [BEHIND / INADEQUATE / LIKE I AM FAILING / OTHER FEELING].

Help me examine this anchor: what are the likely invisible inputs in their outcome — the advantages, timing, luck, or starting conditions I cannot see from the outside? What would a fair-minded assessment of this comparison actually look like if all inputs were visible?
```

---

**How to use:** Be specific about who you are comparing yourself to and what the comparison makes you feel. Vague anchors produce vague audits. The more honestly you describe the person and your reaction, the more useful the examination will be.

**What to watch for:** The invisible inputs section. The article's point is not that the person you are anchored to did not work hard — it is that you are comparing your full journey, including all constraints and starting conditions, to their visible outcome, stripped of context. The audit does not diminish them; it makes the comparison honest.
