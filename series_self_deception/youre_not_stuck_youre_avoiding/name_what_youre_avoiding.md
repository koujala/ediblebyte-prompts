# Name What You're Avoiding

Use this prompt when you have been "stuck" on something for longer than makes sense and the busyness filling that period feels productive but nothing is moving. The prompt asks AI to describe the avoidance pattern from the outside, then hold it up against your situation.

The article's tell: if you already know the answer before you hit send, that is the difference between stuck and avoiding.

---

## Prompt

```
I've been stuck on [SITUATION / PROJECT / DECISION / NEXT STEP] for [TIME PERIOD].

During this time I keep doing [DESCRIBE THE ACTIVITIES FILLING THE STUCK PERIOD — the research, the planning, the side tasks, the conversations that feel related but aren't the thing].

Describe what someone who was avoiding the real next step would look like — specifically, what patterns would appear in their behaviour, what reasoning would they use to explain the delay, and what would the actual avoided thing have in common?

Then tell me: does that match what I just described?
```

---

**How to use:** Describe the stuck period honestly — including the activities that feel legitimate. The activities that fill the avoidance are usually real work; they just are not the work. The response is most useful when it is specific rather than general.

**The test the article offers:** The next step in a genuine stuck situation is unclear. The next step in an avoidance situation is known. After reading the response, ask yourself: do I actually not know the next step, or do I know exactly what it is? That answer is usually immediate.
