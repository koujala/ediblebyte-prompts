# Difficult Conversation Prep — Manager

Use this before any high-stakes feedback conversation with a direct report. Fill in what you know about the person, the situation, and the outcome you need — and get a structured framework to work from instead of a blank page.

---

```
I need to give feedback to a direct report who has been [DESCRIBE THE PATTERN OR ISSUE — e.g., missing deadlines for the past three weeks, withdrawing from team discussions, producing work below their usual standard]. They're [DESCRIBE THE PERSON — e.g., technically strong, one of my best contributors, relatively new to the team] — but something has shifted. In 1:1s they say everything is fine.

I want a direct conversation that names the pattern clearly, opens space for what's actually going on, and is explicit about what needs to change — without damaging the relationship.

Give me:
1. An opening line that sets the tone without putting them on the defensive.
2. Three questions to surface what's actually driving this.
3. Clear language for stating the expectation and the consequence if it continues.
4. A close that leaves them with agency.

Assume they want to perform well and something is getting in the way.
```

---

**How to use this:** Replace the bracketed sections with your actual situation and person. Read the output before the meeting — don't read from it. Edit any language that doesn't sound like you. The structure is the starting point; your judgment about this specific person shapes how you deliver it.

**Adapt it for:** 1:1 agenda prep (describe the person and the open issues), all-hands draft (describe the context and the message), decision documentation (describe what was decided and why), performance review language (describe the behaviour and the impact).
