# Meeting Notes to Status Update

Use this immediately after any project sync or team meeting. Paste your raw notes — get structured action items, decisions, blockers, and a ready-to-send stakeholder update in minutes. What used to take 45 minutes of post-meeting admin takes 5 minutes of reviewing and sending.

---

```
Here are the raw notes from our project sync today: [PASTE YOUR NOTES — bullet points, fragments, or stream of consciousness is fine].

Give me:
1. Decisions made, with who made them.
2. Action items with owner and due date (extract these from the notes; flag any where ownership is unclear).
3. Blockers that need escalation, with a suggested next step for each.
4. A two-paragraph stakeholder update I can send by email.

Tone: direct, no jargon. Assume the reader knows the project but has limited time. Make it shorter and more actionable, not longer and more polished.
```

---

**How to use this:** Run this immediately after every meeting — before you open email. You know you're doing it right when it's your first move after every sync, not an occasional one. Review every item for accuracy; the model can misread context in fragmented notes.

**Extend it for:** Risk registers (describe the project and ask for a risk register with likelihood and impact for each), project charter drafts (paste your bullet-point brief and ask for a full project charter first draft), scope change documentation (describe what changed and why, and ask for a structured scope change request).
