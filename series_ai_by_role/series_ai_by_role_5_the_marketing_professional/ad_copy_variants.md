# Ad Copy Variants

Use this to generate a full range of copy directions before committing to one angle. Describe your product, audience, and campaign goal — get multiple variants across tones and approaches in under a minute. You react, select, refine. The thinking stays yours.

---

```
I'm running a [CHANNEL — e.g., LinkedIn, Meta, Google Display] campaign for [PRODUCT OR SERVICE DESCRIPTION — one or two sentences]. Target audience: [DESCRIBE THE AUDIENCE — role, company size, sector, or demographic].

Give me [NUMBER — e.g., 10] ad copy variants. Vary the approach:
- Some leading with the pain
- Some leading with the outcome
- Some using a specific number or stat
- Some using a question hook
- Some using a bold or provocative angle

For each variant:
- A headline (under [CHARACTER LIMIT — e.g., 60] characters)
- Body copy (under [CHARACTER LIMIT — e.g., 150] characters)
- A one-word label for the angle (e.g., Pain, Outcome, Social Proof, Question, Provocation)
```

---

**How to use this:** Don't approve AI output directly. React to it. Reject what's generic. Mark what's interesting. Ask the model to push the strongest two or three further. Add your brand voice — read every piece before it goes out and edit it until it sounds like your brand, not like a capable but characterless AI assistant.

**Extend it for:** Email sequences (describe the campaign brief and audience, ask for a 3-email sequence with subject lines), campaign briefs (paste your bullet-point brief and ask for a full creative direction document), social captions (share the source content and ask for platform-specific captions for LinkedIn, Instagram, and X with the appropriate character limits).
