# Job Description Draft

Use this to draft a role-specific, jargon-free job description from a brief. A document that used to take 90 minutes to write takes 5 minutes to review and adjust. Apply your legal and policy expertise before publication.

---

```
Write a job description for a [JOB TITLE] at a [COMPANY STAGE AND TYPE — e.g., mid-stage B2B SaaS company, large enterprise retailer, NHS trust]. The role is [LOCATION — e.g., remote, hybrid, on-site in [CITY]].

Focus area: [DESCRIBE THE TEAM OR PRODUCT AREA THE ROLE SITS IN].

Key requirements: [LIST 3–5 REQUIREMENTS — experience level, key skills, domain knowledge].

Tone: direct and specific — no generic filler language like "fast-paced environment" or "wear many hats."

Include:
- A responsibilities section (bullet points, focused on outcomes not activities)
- A requirements section (must-haves separated from nice-to-haves if applicable)
- A short "why join us" section — about the work and the impact, not the perks
```

---

**How to use this:** Apply your HR expertise before this goes anywhere — review for accuracy, compliance, and fit with your company's specific culture and legal requirements. Every AI-generated HR document needs your eyes on it before it reaches a hiring manager, candidate, or legal team.

**Extend it for:** Interview question banks (describe the role and the competencies you're assessing, ask for a structured question bank with follow-up probes), performance review frameworks (describe the role level and the behaviours you're measuring), onboarding checklists (describe the role and team, ask for a first-90-days plan with week-by-week milestones).
