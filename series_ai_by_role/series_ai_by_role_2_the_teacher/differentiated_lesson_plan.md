# Differentiated Lesson Plan

Use this to generate a complete lesson plan with differentiated activities built in. Describe your class, your topic, and your constraints — get a professional starting point in under 2 minutes. You review, adjust for your students' specific needs, and it's yours.

---

```
I'm teaching a [YEAR GROUP / AGE GROUP] [SUBJECT] class. Topic: [TOPIC NAME].

Mixed ability levels — [DESCRIBE YOUR CLASS — e.g., some advanced readers, some struggling; roughly one third each at high, mid, and low attainment].

Create a [LENGTH IN MINUTES]-minute lesson plan with:
- A warm-up question or activity to open
- Differentiated activities for [NUMBER OF ABILITY GROUPS — e.g., three] ability groups
- A closing quiz or exit task to check understanding

[ADD ANY CONSTRAINTS — e.g., no internet access, must link to prior lesson on X, must meet curriculum standard Y]
```

---

**How to use this:** Fill in the bracketed details with your actual class and topic. Read the output before you use it — check for accuracy on the subject matter and adjust anything that doesn't fit your students specifically. The model produces a professional starting point; you make it yours.

**Extend it for:** Quiz question banks at multiple difficulty levels (ask: "Now give me 10 quiz questions on this topic — four easy, four medium, two challenging"), parent update emails (describe the situation and ask for a clear, professional draft), assessment rubrics (describe your learning objectives and ask for a rubric with three performance levels).
