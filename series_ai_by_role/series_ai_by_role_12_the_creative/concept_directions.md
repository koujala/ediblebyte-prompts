# Concept Directions

Use this to generate a range of creative directions from a brief — not to produce the final idea, but to give your creative judgment something to react to. Eight directions in 60 seconds. You reject six. You keep two. You build from there. The blank page is gone; the creative work begins.

---

```
I'm [YOUR ROLE — e.g., creative director / copywriter / brand strategist] working on a campaign for [BRAND OR CLIENT DESCRIPTION — one or two sentences]. Brief: "[PASTE OR SUMMARISE THE CAMPAIGN BRIEF OR CREATIVE CHALLENGE]."

Give me [NUMBER — e.g., 8] creative directions. Vary the approaches:
- Some literal, some abstract
- Some leading with the problem, some with the aspiration
- Some warm, some provocative
- Some using humour, some using tension

For each direction, give me:
- A working title
- A one-line concept in plain language
- A headline
- A visual territory description (what does this look like?)
- The emotional idea — what the audience should feel

Be bold. Don't give me the safe option eight times.
```

---

**How to use this:** Don't approve this output — react to it. The model doesn't know your audience the way you do. It doesn't know what genuinely stops people. Your job is to recognise what's actually interesting in the output and develop it into something that couldn't have come from a model. Select what has potential, push those further, and apply the creative judgment that only you can bring.

**Extend it for:** Copy variant iteration (take the direction you've selected and ask for 10 headline executions exploring that territory), creative rationale (describe the concept and the brief; ask for a clear rationale document to present to the client), campaign naming exploration (describe the brand, campaign, and audience; ask for 20 name or tagline options across different tones).
