# Socratic Concept Explainer

Use this when you hit a concept you didn't fully follow in a lecture or reading. The prompt builds genuine understanding — not a summary you copy without reading. After using it, you should be able to explain the concept without looking at the output.

---

```
I just [had a lecture on / read about / am studying] [TOPIC OR SUBJECT AREA] and I didn't fully follow [SPECIFIC CONCEPT OR PART YOU'RE STUCK ON].

Explain it in plain language — no jargon — like I'm hearing it for the first time. Then give me a concrete example using real numbers or a specific scenario.

Then ask me to explain it back to you in my own words, and tell me if I've understood it correctly. If I haven't, point out specifically where my understanding broke down.
```

---

**How to use this:** This is Socratic learning on demand — not a shortcut. After the model explains it, write your own explanation back without looking. The test: can you explain the concept without the output in front of you? If not, run the loop again. You know you're using it right when AI is making you think harder, not less.

**Verify:** For specific facts, dates, statistics, or citations, always verify independently. Language models produce confident-sounding wrong information. For concept explanation and reasoning — they're excellent. For specific factual claims — check before you rely on them.

**Extend it for:** Essay argument structuring (describe your thesis and evidence; ask for a logical structure to work from), draft critique (paste your essay; ask what the strongest counterargument is), exam prep (ask for one practice question at a time on the topic, grade your reasoning not just your recall).
