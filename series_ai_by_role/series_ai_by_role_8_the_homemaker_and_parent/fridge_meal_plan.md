# Fridge Meal Plan

Use this when you need to make dinner from what's already in the house. No wasted groceries, no extra shopping trip, no 6pm "what's for dinner" problem. List what you actually have and describe the constraints — get practical options back.

---

```
I have [LIST WHAT'S IN THE FRIDGE, FREEZER, AND PANTRY — be specific, e.g., chicken thighs, sweet potato, spinach, garlic, coconut milk, rice, pasta, tinned tomatoes].

Family of [NUMBER] — [DESCRIBE ANY CONSTRAINTS — e.g., one child who won't touch anything green if they can see it, one adult who is vegetarian, someone with a nut allergy].

Give me [NUMBER — e.g., three] dinners and [PACKED LUNCHES / BREAKFASTS / etc.] for [TIMEFRAME — e.g., the next three days] using only what I've listed. Nothing that takes more than [TIME LIMIT — e.g., 45 minutes] on a weeknight.
```

---

**How to use this:** Be specific about what you have — the more accurate the list, the more usable the output. If something in the suggestions won't work for your family, tell the model why and ask it to swap that meal.

**Extend it for:** Weekly meal plans (describe your family, any dietary requirements, and your weekly budget; ask for a full week of dinners with a shopping list), packed lunch variety (describe what your child will and won't eat; ask for 5 different packed lunch ideas).
