# Child Symptom Triage

Use this when your child is unwell and you need a clear, structured starting point — especially out of hours when there is nowhere to turn. This does not replace a doctor. It replaces the panic of having no information, and gives you the right question to ask the GP in the morning.

---

```
My [AGE]-year-old has had [DESCRIBE SYMPTOMS — e.g., a fever of 38.5°C for two days, is eating normally, has a mild cough, and no rash].

What are the likely causes given these symptoms? What should I watch for that would mean going to A&E or calling 999 immediately? What can I do at home tonight to keep them comfortable? And what should I tell the doctor tomorrow morning?
```

---

**How to use this:** Describe the symptoms as specifically as you can — temperature if you have it, duration, what's changed, what seems normal. Read the output as a structured starting point, not a diagnosis. AI is excellent at explaining medical concepts and helping you understand what you might be dealing with. It is not a substitute for clinical judgment.

**Critical rule:** If your child seems seriously unwell — high difficulty breathing, severe lethargy, a rash that doesn't fade under pressure, or your instinct tells you something is wrong — call emergency services or go to A&E. Do not wait for an AI response.

**Extend it for:** Decoding medical letters in plain English (paste the letter and ask what it means in plain language and what questions to ask at the appointment), preparing for a GP appointment (describe the situation and ask what information to bring and what questions to ask).
