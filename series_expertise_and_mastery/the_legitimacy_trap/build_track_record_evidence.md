# Build Track Record Evidence

Use this when you feel like you do not have the credentials to speak authoritatively in a domain, but you have the experience. The prompt takes what you have actually done and surfaces what a fair-minded outsider would conclude from it — independent of titles, degrees, or institutional affiliation. The track record is the credential. This prompt helps you see it that way.

```
I have [X YEARS] of experience in [DOMAIN] but I struggle to feel legitimate enough to [SPEAK PUBLICLY ABOUT THIS / WRITE AUTHORITATIVELY / LEAD THIS INITIATIVE / APPLY FOR THIS ROLE].

Here is what I have actually done:
[LIST SPECIFIC WORK, DECISIONS, AND OUTCOMES — be concrete: systems built, problems solved, decisions that held, teams developed, results achieved]

Help me identify which of these constitutes genuine evidence of expertise — not credentials, but track record. What would a fair-minded outsider conclude from this list?
```

**How to use the output:** The AI's assessment is a mirror, not a verdict. Use it to see your own work the way someone without your internal narrative would see it. If the output surprises you — if it reads as more substantial than it felt from the inside — that gap between what you have done and how you have been holding it is the legitimacy trap the article describes.
