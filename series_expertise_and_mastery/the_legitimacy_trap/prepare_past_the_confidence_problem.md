# Prepare Past the Confidence Problem

Use this when you have been invited to speak, write, or lead on something in your domain and the thing stopping you feels like confidence rather than capability. The prompt runs you through a compressed equivalent of a hundred dry runs — pushback on the weakest parts, the hardest questions, repeat until the content is solid. When preparation is no longer the issue, what remains is just the doing.

```
I have been invited to [SPEAK AT / WRITE ABOUT / LEAD] [SOMETHING IN YOUR DOMAIN — e.g. a conference session on production ML systems / an article on distributed architecture trade-offs / a strategic initiative I have not led before] and I do not feel ready.

Help me prepare until preparation is not the issue anymore.

Here is what I plan to say / do:
[OUTLINE YOUR CONTENT OR APPROACH]

Push back on the weakest parts. Ask me the hardest questions the audience will ask. Keep going until I have answered them all. I want to walk in with the content, not the confidence problem.
```

**How to use the output:** Do not just read the AI's pushback — answer it, out loud or in writing, before moving to the next question. If you cannot answer a question the AI raises, that is a genuine gap to close before the real moment. If you can answer all of them, the confidence problem has been correctly diagnosed: it was never about preparation.
