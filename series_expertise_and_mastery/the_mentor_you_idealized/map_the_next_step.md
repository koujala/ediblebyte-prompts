# Map the Next Step

Use this when you have been circling a next step — a pitch, a publication, a role, a conversation — and keep finding reasons to wait. The prompt forces the step into a concrete map: what it requires, what the obstacles are, whether readiness is actually the bottleneck. Often it is not. Often the bottleneck is the absence of someone else's permission, which is not coming.

```
I'm about to take [SPECIFIC NEXT STEP — e.g. pitch my work publicly / apply for a senior role / start writing the series I've been planning] but I keep finding reasons to wait.

Map out exactly what this step requires — the skills, the decisions, the likely obstacles. Be specific and concrete, not reassuring.

I want to see whether readiness is actually the issue. If it is not, I want to know what the actual issue is.
```

**How to use the output:** If the map shows genuine gaps in readiness — specific skills or knowledge you do not yet have — those are things to build. If the map shows everything is in place and the obstacles are about how it might be received, that is not a readiness problem. That is the permission problem the article describes. The next step is the one you take without the permission.
