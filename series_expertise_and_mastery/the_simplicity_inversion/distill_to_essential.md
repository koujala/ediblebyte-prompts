# Distill to Essential

Use this before presenting a complex recommendation to a leadership or non-specialist audience. The structure — one sentence recommendation, two sentences of why, one sentence of what it costs — forces the Curse of Knowledge in reverse: you communicate at the simplicity level of their need, not the complexity level of your acquisition. The depth is available on request; this is the entry point.

```
I need to present [COMPLEX TOPIC OR RECOMMENDATION — e.g. our proposed move to a microservices architecture / the findings from the six-month vendor evaluation] to a leadership audience in two minutes.

Here is everything I know about it:
[EXPLAIN AT YOUR NORMAL LEVEL OF DEPTH — include context, caveats, trade-offs, edge cases]

Now help me distill this to:
- One sentence recommendation
- Two sentences of why
- One sentence of what it costs or requires

No caveats, no edge cases — just the essential version. I will bring the depth if they ask for it.
```

**How to use the output:** Read the distilled version aloud. If it feels uncomfortably thin — if you feel the urge to add "but it's more complicated than that" — that is the Curse of Knowledge resisting. The discomfort is not a signal to add complexity. It is a signal that the exercise is working. Lead with the simple version; bring the complexity only when asked.
