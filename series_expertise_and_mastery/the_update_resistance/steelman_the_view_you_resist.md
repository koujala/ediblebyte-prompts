# Steelman the View You Are Resisting

Use this when you have been resisting an idea, approach, or change — and your objections have become the endpoint of the conversation rather than the beginning of an examination. The key instruction is: do not soften it, do not acknowledge your objections. Just make the best possible case for the thing being resisted. This removes the most common escape route: dismissing the person making the argument rather than answering the argument itself.

```
I have been resisting [IDEA / APPROACH / CHANGE — e.g. moving to a streaming architecture / adopting a new management framework / changing the technical stack] for [ROUGHLY HOW LONG].

My objections are:
[LIST YOUR OBJECTIONS — be honest and specific]

I want you to steelman the opposing position as strongly as possible — do not soften it, do not acknowledge my objections, just make the best possible case for the thing I am resisting.

I will tell you afterward whether any of it lands.
```

**How to use the output:** Read each point before composing a rebuttal. For each one, ask: am I dismissing this because the argument is weak, or because engaging with it would require updating something I have invested years in? The distinction matters. If the steelman lands — even partially — that is information worth acting on.
