# Audit Your Mental Model

Use this when you want to test whether the expertise you have built over years is still calibrated to current reality, or whether you have been defending an old map of new territory. The earlier your mental model was formed, the more important this audit becomes — not to undermine the expertise, but to find the edges where it needs revision.

```
I have deep expertise in [DOMAIN] built over [X YEARS]. I want to identify where my mental model might be outdated.

Here are the core assumptions my model is built on:
[LIST 3-5 KEY BELIEFS OR ASSUMPTIONS — e.g. "relational databases are the right default for most production systems" / "the first six months is when a new hire's trajectory is set"]

For each one, tell me:
1. Is this still considered best practice, or has the field moved?
2. What has changed that most affects this assumption?
3. Where am I most likely to be defending an old map of new territory?
```

**How to use the output:** Treat this as a calibration exercise, not a verdict. The goal is to find which assumptions have held and which have eroded so you can update selectively — not abandon the expertise, but upgrade the parts that have drifted. Beliefs that survive scrutiny can be held with more confidence. The ones that don't survive are worth revisiting with someone current in the field.
