# Beginner's Mind in an Adjacent Domain

Use this when you are about to make a decision in a domain that borders your expertise — and you notice yourself carrying confidence from your main area into the adjacent one without checking whether it applies. The prompt surfaces what you think you know that might actually be wrong, and replaces assumption with the right questions to ask.

```
I am considered an expert in [DOMAIN — e.g. machine learning systems / financial modelling / organisational design].

I am about to make a decision that touches [ADJACENT AREA — e.g. data governance and regulatory compliance / operational logistics / employment law].

I want to approach that adjacent area with genuine beginner's mind rather than borrowed confidence from my main expertise.

Help me identify:
1. What do I think I know about [ADJACENT AREA] that might actually be wrong?
2. What questions should I be asking rather than assumptions I should be making?
```

**How to use the output:** The point of naming assumptions explicitly is to make them visible before they cause a problem — not after. For each assumption the AI flags, treat it as provisional until you have spoken to someone with genuine expertise in that adjacent domain. The expert in Dr. Chen's story was not wrong about medicine. She was wrong about which domain the question belonged to.
