# Scan for Adjacent Blind Spots

Use this before finalising a decision that touches domains beyond your main expertise. AI does not know which domain is "the real domain," so it does not assume the question belongs only to the expert's area — it surfaces adjacent considerations that a single-domain expert may not see. This is the function that most often fails in high-stakes decisions: nobody with cross-domain visibility in the room.

```
I am a [ROLE / DOMAIN EXPERT — e.g. senior cardiologist / principal engineer / head of product] making a decision about [DESCRIBE THE DECISION — including context, what is being chosen, what is at stake].

I want to stress-test this decision across domains I may not be seeing clearly.

Identify the three most likely adjacent domains where this decision could go wrong — technical, legal, social, operational, ethical, or whatever is most relevant. For each one, give me the specific question I should be asking someone with expertise in that domain before I proceed.
```

**How to use the output:** For each adjacent domain the AI surfaces, ask yourself: do I have someone with genuine expertise in that domain involved in this decision? If not, the output gives you the specific question to bring to someone who does. The goal is not to become an expert in adjacent domains — it is to know the right question to ask the person who already is.
