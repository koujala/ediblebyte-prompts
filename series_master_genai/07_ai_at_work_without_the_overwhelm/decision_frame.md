# Decision Frame

AI is one of the most valuable tools available for *framing* a hard decision — and one of the worst tools for making one. This prompt gets you structure, surfaced trade-offs, and the single most useful question to answer before you commit. The call stays yours.

```
I need to make a decision. Help me frame it — do not make it for me.

The decision: [DESCRIBE IT CLEARLY — what you are deciding, by when]

Options I am currently considering:
- [OPTION A]
- [OPTION B]
- [OPTION C, if any]

Key constraints:
- Time: [...]
- Budget: [...]
- Relationships: [...]
- Risk tolerance: [...]

People affected by this decision, and their likely perspectives:
[LIST — each person / group and how you think they'll view this]

Before I decide, help me by:
1. Identifying any options I may not have considered — especially obvious ones I might be overlooking, or unconventional ones worth naming even if we don't pick them.
2. Surfacing the single most significant trade-off in each option I listed — the thing I am implicitly giving up if I choose it.
3. Telling me what information, if I had it, would most change my thinking. Name the top two gaps.
4. Giving me the single question I should be able to answer honestly before I commit to any of these options.

Do not tell me what to choose. Do not lean toward one option. The decision, the accountability, and the relationships are mine.
```

**What AI cannot do here:** Make the call. Take the accountability. Know the history, the unspoken dynamics, the relationships that live outside the prompt. Those are yours. Keep them.
