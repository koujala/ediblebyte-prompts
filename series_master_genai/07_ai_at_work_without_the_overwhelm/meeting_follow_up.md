# Meeting Follow-Up

Meetings are only valuable if the decisions stick and the actions actually happen. A follow-up produced immediately is worth three times the value of one sent two days later. This prompt turns a messy brain-dump of notes into a clean follow-up in ninety seconds.

```
I just finished a meeting. I want to produce a clean follow-up while it's still fresh.

Topic: [WHAT THE MEETING WAS ABOUT]

Attendees and their roles:
[NAMES / ROLES]

My rough notes (stream of consciousness — messy is fine):
[PASTE RAW NOTES — what was discussed, what was decided, who said they would do what, anything that felt unresolved]

Please produce, in this exact structure:

1. **Summary** — a concise paragraph of what was discussed (maximum 5 sentences, no fluff).
2. **Decisions made** — a clear bulleted list of the decisions, phrased unambiguously.
3. **Action items** — a numbered list, each formatted as:
   [Owner] — [what they will do] — [by when, if stated]
4. **Open questions** — anything that was raised but not resolved, so it doesn't get lost.

Format the whole thing so it can be sent directly as a follow-up email, with a short greeting line at the top and a single-sentence sign-off. Keep it professional and human — no corporate padding.
```

**Tip:** Review and personalise before sending. Add the one thing only you, as a participant, would know needs to be in there — an acknowledgement, a concern, a thank-you. The structure comes from AI; the relational layer comes from you.
