# Write in My Voice

Generic AI writing has a recognisable texture: balanced, professional, slightly formal, nobody's voice in particular. Getting AI to write in your voice requires one thing above all — examples.

## Prompt — With samples (strongly preferred)

```
I want you to write something in my voice. First, study me.

Here are three samples of writing I have produced:

--- Sample 1 ---
[PASTE A WRITING SAMPLE]

--- Sample 2 ---
[PASTE A DIFFERENT WRITING SAMPLE]

--- Sample 3 ---
[PASTE A THIRD SAMPLE]

Study:
- sentence length and rhythm
- vocabulary — which words I reach for, and which I avoid
- tone — how formal, how warm, how direct
- structural habits — how I open, how I close, how I use paragraphs and lists

Now write [THE ACTUAL TASK — post, email, section, etc.].
Requirements:
- Length: [WORD COUNT]
- Purpose: [WHAT YOU WANT IT TO DO]
- Audience: [WHO IS READING]

The output should sound like me, not like a press release and not like the AI default voice. Where you're unsure about a stylistic choice, default to shorter sentences and a more direct tone.
```

## Prompt — Without samples (describe your voice)

When you don't have samples handy, describe your voice explicitly:

```
Before you write, here is my voice:
- [TRAIT — e.g. "direct and conversational"]
- [TRAIT — e.g. "short sentences, very little hedging"]
- [TRAIT — e.g. "I avoid corporate jargon and 'excited to announce'-style language"]
- [TRAIT — e.g. "I sometimes use a dry observation to make a point land"]
- [TRAIT — e.g. "I write to be understood, not to sound impressive"]

Now write [THE ACTUAL TASK] in that voice.
```

**Tip:** Save your three best samples somewhere easy to paste. Once you have them, every "write in my voice" prompt is ten seconds away.
