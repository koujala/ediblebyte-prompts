# Difficult Message

For messages that need to be honest without being demoralising: critical feedback, bad news, a hard "no", a redo request. The goal is to be direct, specific, and to end with a clear path forward.

```
I need to send a difficult message. Help me draft it — direct but not demoralising.

The situation:
[DESCRIBE HONESTLY — what happened, what was supposed to happen, why this needs addressing]

Who I'm sending it to:
[NAME / ROLE, and the nature of our working relationship — new, long-standing, senior, peer, report]

What specifically needs to land:
[THE HARD THING — e.g. "the work missed the mark and needs to be redone", "we're not moving forward", "the deadline isn't realistic"]

What I do NOT want:
- to sound passive-aggressive
- to bury the hard part in softening language
- to over-apologise for something that simply needs to be said
- to be cold or performative about the fact that I'm "being direct"

What I DO want:
- acknowledge the effort / context briefly if warranted, but do not lead with it
- be specific about what the issue actually is (not a vague "it didn't quite work")
- end with a clear, concrete next step — what I need from them, by when
- leave the relationship intact

Draft it. Keep it [SHORT / MEDIUM] in length. Match a [WARM-BUT-DIRECT / NEUTRAL-PROFESSIONAL] tone.
```

**How to use the output:** Treat this as a *draft you will personalise*, not a message you will send as-is. Rewrite the opening line and the closing in your own words — those two sentences are what the reader will remember.
