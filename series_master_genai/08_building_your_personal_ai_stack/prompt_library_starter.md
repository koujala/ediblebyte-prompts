# Prompt Library Starter

A personal prompt library is a collection of prompts you've developed, tested, and refined for your recurring tasks. The investment is front-loaded — writing and refining a good prompt takes twenty to thirty minutes the first time, and thirty seconds every time after. A library of ten good prompts beats any new tool you sign up for this month.

This is a meta-prompt: use it to build your first five.

```
I'm building the first version of my personal AI prompt library. Help me design it.

Step 1 — surface the right tasks.

Ask me to list my five most recurring tasks — the ones I do every week or more. Not the most exciting use cases. The most frequent ones. If my first list is heavy on the novel or aspirational, push back and ask what I actually do on a typical Tuesday.

Step 2 — for each of the five tasks, produce a starter prompt using the Role + Task + Context + Format structure:

Role: [who the AI should be for this specific task]
Task: [the specific, directive action]
Context: [the recurring situational details I would otherwise have to retype every time — audience, constraints, what "good" looks like, what to avoid]
Format: [the output shape I need]

For each prompt, also give me:
- the one variable I will have to fill in fresh every single time (mark it clearly with [BRACKETS])
- the single most common way this prompt will probably fall short on first run
- one follow-up line I can send if that failure shows up

Step 3 — at the end, give me a short checklist for refining each prompt:
- what to look for in the first real-run output
- how to tell whether the prompt needs more context, sharper format, or a different role
- when to stop refining (the signal that the prompt is "good enough to keep")

Keep the structure clean. I will be pasting these into a saved-prompts store and reusing them every week.
```

**Tip:** Store the finished prompts somewhere you can open in under fifteen seconds. If retrieval takes longer than that, the library won't become a habit — and un-used prompts are just text files.
