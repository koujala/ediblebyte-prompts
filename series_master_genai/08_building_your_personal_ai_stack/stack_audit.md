# 90-Day Stack Audit

Use this when you've been using AI tools for a while and have a vague sense they're useful but can't quantify it. The prompt gives you five honest questions — not a pat on the back — to surface whether your stack is producing real value, where the gaps are, and the one change that would matter most.

```
I have been using AI tools for roughly the past 90 days, primarily for:
[LIST YOUR ACTUAL USE CASES — e.g. "meeting follow-ups, first drafts of emails, weekly planning, occasional research"]

The tools currently in my stack:
- Core: [PRIMARY TOOL]
- Specialists: [ANY SECONDARY TOOLS]
- Saved prompts: [ROUGH COUNT / WHAT THEY COVER]
- Habit attachments: [WHEN IN THE DAY I REACH FOR AI WITHOUT THINKING ABOUT IT]

I want an honest self-assessment, not a summary of my own report.

Build me a short self-assessment framework — exactly five questions — that will tell me:
1. **Time** — whether any tasks are meaningfully faster than 90 days ago, and whether AI is actually the reason.
2. **Quality** — whether the outputs I'm producing are genuinely better, or just faster versions of the same quality.
3. **Friction** — whether any part of my AI workflow I consistently avoid or shortcut is signalling that something isn't working.
4. **Coverage** — whether there are high-value recurring tasks where I'm still not using AI at all, and whether that's a deliberate choice or an oversight.
5. **Bloat** — whether any tool or prompt has earned its place through use, or is just sitting in my stack out of inertia.

For each question:
- phrase it so it demands a specific, verifiable answer (not "yes, it's working")
- tell me what different honest answers to it would mean
- flag one answer I'm probably tempted to give that I should be suspicious of

At the end, ask me: *of the five answers, which one change would have the highest impact on my results over the next 90 days?* Do not answer that question — leave it for me.
```

**Tip:** Run this again in 90 days. The gap between audits is where the compounding happens.
