# Habit Triggers

Habits form when a behaviour is attached to a trigger that already exists. The most effective way to integrate AI is to attach your key prompts to moments that already happen in your day — not to add new time, but to fill existing moments with better intention.

Use this prompt to design your own trigger map.

```
Help me design a set of habit triggers for my AI use.

Describe to me four triggers I already have in my day that are good candidates for AI prompts, and for each one suggest a specific prompt from the library I'm building:

1. **Start of day** — the moment I sit down and open my laptop. Before the inbox. Suggest what prompt fires here and what outcome I should expect from it.
2. **Before any significant meeting** — when my calendar alert fires. Suggest the prompt that should fire, and how short it should be to not eat my prep time.
3. **After any significant meeting** — the moment I close my notes. Suggest the prompt that should fire while the conversation is still fresh.
4. **End of day** — the moment I start to close down. Suggest the prompt that should fire to clear my head before I shut the laptop.

For each trigger, also tell me:
- the specific physical or calendar moment that is the *actual* cue (not "after lunch" — something more concrete)
- roughly how long the prompt should take to complete (so I don't skip it when I'm busy)
- the single sign that the habit is working — what I will notice after two weeks of doing it
- the single failure mode — what I will do when the habit is breaking down (skipping it, rushing it, using it ritualistically without producing useful output)

Keep it concrete. I want something I could print on a sticky note and tape to my laptop.
```

**The rule:** None of these require new time. They require attaching a prompt to a moment that already exists. When you find yourself saying "I don't have time for this" — you're describing a trigger problem, not a time problem.
