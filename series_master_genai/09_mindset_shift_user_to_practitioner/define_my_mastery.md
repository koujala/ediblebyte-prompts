# Define My Mastery

Mastery is not a universal destination. It is a personal one — defined by your specific work, your specific goals, and the specific problems you are trying to solve. This prompt helps you name it, specifically, so "getting better at AI" stops being a vague ambition and becomes a concrete direction.

```
I have worked through a foundational series on generative AI. I now want to define what meaningful AI mastery actually looks like for me — not as a generic goal, but tied to my specific work, life, and priorities.

Here is what you need to know about me:

My work / role:
[DESCRIBE — what you actually do, day to day]

What I am trying to be better at (independent of AI):
[THE REAL GOAL — the professional or personal capability you're trying to grow into]

Where AI has already earned its place in my routine:
[WHERE IT'S WORKING — specific use cases, habits, prompts]

Where I think AI could help me but I haven't built the habit yet:
[WHERE THE OPPORTUNITY IS]

My honest constraints:
[TIME / ENERGY / FOCUS / TOOLING]

Based on all of that, help me answer four questions:

1. **What does meaningful AI mastery look like for me specifically?** Not buzzwords. A specific picture of what I'd be capable of — in my work, my thinking, my routines — if I were genuinely fluent. Describe the day-in-the-life of that version of me.

2. **What does that version of me enable that I cannot do today?** Name one professional outcome and one personal outcome that become possible with that capability but aren't currently.

3. **What would I need to build, practise, and develop over the next 12 months to get there?** Stack, prompts, habits, judgment — be specific, and keep the list short enough that I will actually do it.

4. **What is the single most important thing I should focus on in the next 90 days?** One thing. The keystone. The one that, if I got it genuinely solid, would pull the rest along.

Do not dilute the answers to be comfortable. Dilute them if they'd be too much to actually do.
```

**How to use the output:** Save it. Reread it in 30, 60, and 90 days. The point is not to produce a perfect plan — it's to catch yourself, in 90 days, doing something different because you wrote this down.
