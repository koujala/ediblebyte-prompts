# Intuition Log

AI intuition — the ability to look at a task and immediately know whether AI belongs, what the prompt needs to contain, and what good output should look like — is built the same way any professional intuition is: by doing the work, noticing what happened, and adjusting.

This is a weekly (or fortnightly) reflection prompt. It turns your recent AI use into deliberate practice.

```
I want to reflect on how I've been using AI recently, to sharpen my intuition rather than just accumulate usage.

Here are examples from the last [WEEK / TWO WEEKS]:

**Prompts that worked exceptionally well:**
[LIST 1–3 EXAMPLES — roughly what you asked, and why the output actually helped]

**Prompts that disappointed me:**
[LIST 1–3 EXAMPLES — what you asked, and what fell short]

**Times I reached for AI when I shouldn't have:**
[ANY CASES WHERE AI WAS THE WRONG TOOL, OR WHERE I OUTSOURCED JUDGMENT I SHOULD HAVE KEPT]

**Times I should have reached for AI and didn't:**
[TASKS WHERE I DID THE WORK THE HARD WAY BECAUSE THE HABIT WASN'T THERE]

Help me reflect:

1. **Patterns in what's working** — what do the successful prompts have in common? Structure, specificity, context, tone, use of examples? Name two patterns I can deliberately repeat.

2. **Patterns in what's failing** — what do the disappointing prompts have in common? Was it missing context, vague task, wrong role, unrealistic expectation? Name the single most common failure mode I'm showing.

3. **Boundary check** — am I keeping AI on the right side of the line between "supports my thinking" and "replaces my thinking"? Flag anything from my examples that suggests I'm drifting toward outsourcing.

4. **The one adjustment** — based on everything above, what is the single specific change I should make to my prompting, my workflow, or my habits in the next two weeks? Just one.
```

**The practitioner's question:** Not *"Did AI give me something useful?"* but *"Did I give AI enough to work with — and did I bring enough judgment to what came back?"*

Run this enough times and that question starts firing automatically. That's the intuition.
