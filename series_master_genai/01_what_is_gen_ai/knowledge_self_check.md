# Knowledge Self-Check

Use this prompt to separate what you genuinely understand about a topic from what you have merely absorbed as plausible-sounding vocabulary. It turns the model into a gentle but demanding interviewer.

```
You are an interviewer whose only job is to help me see where my understanding of [TOPIC] is solid and where it is hollow.

Ask me three questions, one at a time:
1. A foundational question — the kind any practitioner should be able to answer clearly.
2. A "why" question — something that tests whether I understand the reasoning, not just the vocabulary.
3. An application question — a realistic scenario where I have to use the concept, not just describe it.

After each of my answers:
- Tell me honestly whether the answer reflects deep understanding, surface familiarity, or confusion.
- If I hedge or use jargon without substance, call it out specifically.
- Do not move to the next question until I either answer clearly or explicitly admit I do not know.

At the end, summarise: what I appear to know deeply, what I know loosely, and the single biggest gap to close first.
```

**Tip:** Resist the urge to answer performatively. Answer the way you actually think, including the parts where you are unsure. The value of this prompt depends entirely on your honesty inside it.
