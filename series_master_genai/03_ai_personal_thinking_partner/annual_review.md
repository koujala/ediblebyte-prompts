# Honest Annual Review

A structured review across the four areas most people never make time to look at clearly: career, relationships, health, and personal growth. The point of this prompt is not to produce a tidy summary — it's to catch yourself in the rationalisations you would otherwise let slide.

```
I want to do an honest annual review of my life across four areas: career, relationships, health, and personal growth.

For each area, follow this exact structure:
1. Ask me one question at a time — not a list. Wait for my answer before moving on.
2. After I answer, reflect back what you heard in your own words.
3. Flag anything in my answer that sounds like a rationalisation, a hedge, or a sanitised version of something more uncomfortable.
4. Ask one follow-up question that digs into what I may be avoiding.
5. Do not move to the next area until I have said something that feels genuinely honest rather than just acceptable.

Order of areas:
1. Career
2. Relationships
3. Health
4. Personal growth

At the very end, once all four areas are complete:
- Summarise the single most honest thing I said in each area.
- Name the one theme that shows up across more than one area — the thing that, if I addressed it, would improve multiple parts of my life at once.
- Give me one commitment for the next 90 days that directly addresses that theme. One. Not five.
```

**How to use the output:** The quality of this process depends entirely on your willingness to be honest *inside* the prompt. If you notice yourself writing an answer that sounds like a LinkedIn post, stop and rewrite it the way you would say it to a trusted friend at 11pm.
