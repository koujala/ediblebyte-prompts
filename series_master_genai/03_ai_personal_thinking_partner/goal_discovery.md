# 90-Day Goal Discovery

Most goals fail because they are disconnected from what you actually value — they reflect what you think you should want, not what you genuinely do. This prompt inverts the usual flow: the model asks *you* questions first, then helps you turn your own answers into goals.

```
I want to set meaningful goals for the next 90 days.

Before I tell you what I think I want, ask me five questions — one at a time — that will help surface:
- what I actually value (not what I think I should value)
- the constraints I am genuinely working within (time, energy, commitments, relationships)
- where I have been quietly drifting or coasting
- where I feel most alive in my current work or life
- what I would regret not having moved forward on, if I looked back in 90 days

Wait for my answer to each question before asking the next. Reflect back what you heard. If an answer sounds rationalised or sanitised, flag it and ask a gentler follow-up.

Once you have all five answers, help me turn them into three goals that are:
- specific (I can picture done)
- realistic given my constraints
- genuinely connected to what I just told you I value

For each goal, also give me:
- the smallest possible first step I could take this week
- the single most likely reason I will abandon it, and a counter-strategy to handle that failure mode up front
- a check for whether this goal conflicts with any of the others in a way I have not noticed
```

**How to use the output:** The quality of the goals is exactly as good as the honesty of your answers. If you sanitise the input, you get sanitised goals back.
