# Steelman a Decision

When you feel certain about a decision, that certainty often means you have stopped examining the alternative fairly. Use this prompt when you are already leaning strongly toward one option — it forces you to face the strongest version of the counterargument, not the weakest.

```
I am facing a decision and I am already leaning toward one option. Before I commit, I want to stress-test that preference.

The decision: [DESCRIBE THE DECISION IN FULL — background, context, stakes]

The option I am leaning toward: [OPTION A]
The option I am leaning away from: [OPTION B]

My current reasoning for A is: [YOUR REASONING, HONESTLY]

Now do the following:
1. Make the strongest possible case for Option B — the version a thoughtful, well-informed advocate of B would make. No strawmen, no weak versions.
2. Identify the single most important thing about B that my current reasoning seems to overlook or underweight.
3. Tell me: if I still choose A after seeing this, what assumption in my reasoning most needs to be true for that choice to hold up?

Do not tell me what to decide. The decision is mine.
```

**How to use the output:** If your preference for A survives the strongest version of B, you can proceed with genuine confidence rather than untested conviction. If it doesn't, you just saved yourself a bad call.
