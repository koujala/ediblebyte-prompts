# Pre-Meeting Prep

A two-minute prompt that turns you into the most prepared person in the room. Use it when your calendar alert fires — the alert is the trigger.

```
I have a meeting in [TIMEFRAME — e.g. "one hour"] about [TOPIC / DECISION / PROJECT].

Attendees and their roles:
[LIST NAMES AND ROLES, AND YOUR RELATIONSHIP IF RELEVANT]

The stated objective of the meeting: [WHAT THE AGENDA SAYS]

The real objective (what I actually need): [WHAT YOU NEED TO LEAVE WITH]

Likely points of tension or disagreement: [WHERE YOU EXPECT PUSHBACK, OR WHAT IS POLITICALLY SENSITIVE]

Help me prepare:
1. The two or three points I most need to make clearly — phrased the way I would actually say them.
2. The questions I should ask to move the conversation forward, rather than just react to it.
3. The single objection I am most likely to face, and one honest way I might respond to it.
4. The single outcome I need to leave the room with — if I don't get this, the meeting was not a success.
5. One thing I should consciously *not* do in this meeting (over-explain, get defensive, agree too quickly, etc.).

Keep it short. I need this for reading, not reciting.
```

**Tip:** Treat the output as a thinking aid, not a script. The goal is to walk in clear, not rehearsed.
