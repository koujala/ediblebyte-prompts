# End-of-Day Shutdown

Two prompts for closing the day cleanly: one to pre-process distractions *before* a focus block, and one to close the mental tabs at day's end so your evening is actually yours.

---

## Prompt A — Pre-process distractions (before a focus block)

Use this in the fifteen minutes before you close your inbox and go into a deep work session.

```
I'm about to go into a focused work block. Before I close my inbox, help me clear the queue so nothing pulls at my attention during deep work.

Here are the messages and requests currently waiting for a response:
[PASTE OR SUMMARISE — emails, Slacks, DMs, open threads]

For each one:
1. If a quick, low-judgment reply is possible, draft it in one or two lines so I can send it in seconds after a quick review.
2. If it actually requires my considered judgment, flag it clearly and add it to a "handle after the focus block" list — do not try to answer it for me.
3. If it can be deferred without real consequence (to tomorrow, to next week, or indefinitely), say so explicitly and suggest a rough when.

At the end, give me a short summary: how many I can send now, how many are parked for later, and the one item I should NOT forget once the focus block ends.
```

---

## Prompt B — End-of-day brain dump

Use this before you close the laptop for the day.

```
I'm ending my workday. Here's everything still on my mind:

[DUMP EVERYTHING — unfinished tasks, things that went wrong, decisions I haven't made, people I'm waiting on, things I'm worried about for tomorrow, nagging thoughts, small admin I keep putting off]

Help me:
1. Capture everything so nothing is lost overnight — organise the list into a shape I can pick up cold tomorrow morning.
2. Identify the two or three things that genuinely need to be first on tomorrow's list, and say why each one earns that spot.
3. Flag anything I am carrying that I cannot actually do anything about until tomorrow — so I can consciously put it down for the evening.
4. Name anything I wrote that sounds like a worry rather than a task. Worries go on a separate shortlist, not the action list.
```

**Tip:** After Prompt B, close the laptop deliberately. Tomorrow has a starting point. The evening is yours.
