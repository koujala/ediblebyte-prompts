# Morning Plan

Most people plan their day reactively — open the inbox, see what's waiting, and let the loudest thing set the agenda. This prompt replaces reaction with intention, before the inbox gets a vote.

```
It is the start of my workday. Help me build a realistic hour-by-hour plan.

Everything on my plate today:
[LIST TASKS, MEETINGS, AND COMMITMENTS — messy is fine]

My single most important outcome for today: [X]

My energy and focus are typically best in the: [MORNING / AFTERNOON]

Fixed commitments I cannot move:
[LIST MEETINGS AND CALLS WITH TIMES]

Hours I am genuinely available today: [START – END]

Build me a realistic hour-by-hour plan that:
- protects a block of uninterrupted time for my most important outcome, placed in my best-energy window
- sequences the remaining tasks by energy requirement (demanding work when I'm sharp, low-cognitive-load work when I'm not)
- flags anything I should defer to another day or drop entirely, with a one-sentence rationale
- leaves at least one small buffer for things that will inevitably overrun

Keep the plan concrete — time blocks with what I will actually do in each one.
```

**How to use the output:** The plan AI produces will not be perfect. Your day will not follow it exactly. The point is that you start with intention rather than drift — and intention, even imperfect, produces better outcomes than reaction.
