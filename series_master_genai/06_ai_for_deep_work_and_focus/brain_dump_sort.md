# Brain Dump Sort

The brain dump is one of the oldest productivity techniques — and AI makes it significantly more useful. The goal is not to build a to-do list. It's to get every open loop out of your working memory so you can think clearly again.

```
I'm going to dump everything that's currently in my head. Your job is to sort it, not filter it.

Here's the dump — messy is fine, order doesn't matter, don't skip anything:

[PASTE EVERYTHING — tasks, worries, ideas, nagging thoughts, half-decisions, things you're putting off, things someone else is waiting on, things you feel guilty about, things you keep meaning to mention]

Sort what I wrote into four clear categories:

1. **Act this week** — things that genuinely need action from me in the next few days.
2. **Can wait** — real tasks, but not this week; give me a rough "when" for each if possible.
3. **Worrying, but can't act on right now** — things I'm carrying mentally that I have no way of doing anything about at this moment.
4. **Probably let go of** — things that don't actually matter, aren't mine to solve, or are old thoughts I've been dragging along.

Reflect back what you see. Don't judge. Don't filter anything out — if something is on the list, it belongs in exactly one of the four categories.

End with: the one thing from category 1 that is clearly most important, and the one thing from category 3 that is eating the most mental space.
```

**Why category 3 matters most:** Most cognitive load doesn't come from things you need to do. It comes from things you're carrying mentally that you can't act on right now. Naming them and parking them — consciously, explicitly — frees up more mental space than completing ten small tasks.
