# Structured Learning Plan

Most people start learning without a plan. Without structure, you don't know what you don't know, can't measure progress, and have no way of knowing when you're done. This prompt builds the plan around *your real constraints*, not aspirational ones.

```
I want to learn [TOPIC].

My current knowledge level: [BEGINNER / I KNOW X BUT NOT Y / INTERMEDIATE — BE SPECIFIC]
My goal: [SPECIFIC OUTCOME — "be able to do X" beats "understand Y"]
Time I honestly have available: [X HOURS PER WEEK — THE REAL NUMBER, NOT THE ASPIRATIONAL ONE]
How I learn best: [READING / DOING / WATCHING / A MIX — AND WHICH MIX]

Build me a structured learning plan with:
1. The right sequence of topics, from foundation to goal — explain briefly why each one has to come in the order you've chosen.
2. Milestones along the way: concrete "I can now do X" checkpoints, not just "I've studied X."
3. A way to test myself at each stage — a small exercise, project, or quiz that proves the milestone is real.
4. An honest estimate of how long each stage should take given my weekly hours.
5. Resources or types of resource that fit my learning style (not a dump of every possible link — a short, curated suggestion).
6. One signal that would tell me, mid-plan, that I have misjudged my starting level and should adjust.
```

## Starter (30-day) variant

When you want a smaller commitment to get moving:

```
I want to learn [TOPIC]. My current level is [X]. My goal is [Y]. I have [Z HOURS PER WEEK].

Ask me three questions to understand how I learn best. Then build me a realistic 30-day starter plan — just the first 30 days. Small enough to start. Structured enough to continue.
```

**Tip:** Always include your *real* time constraint. A plan built around "2 hours a day" that you actually have 30 minutes for will fail on day three.
